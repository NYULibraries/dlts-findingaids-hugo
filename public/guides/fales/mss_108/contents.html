Series II: New Yorker Assignments

Scope and Contents note

Comprised of research, manuscripts and correspondence related to two proposed articles on the Einstein family for The New Yorker (neither of which was published): one on Lieserl Einstein, the subject of Einstein's Daughter, and one on the legal battles of Evelyn Einstein, with whom Zackheim struck up a friendship during her research on the family. Arranged in four subseries.

Series III: Einstein

Scope and Contents note

Contains materials related to the production of Zackheim's second novel, Einstein's Daughter: The Search for Lieserl, a biography of Einstein's illegitimate daughter, conceived out of wedlock when Einstein and Mileva Maric were students at Zurich Polytechnic Institute. These files were collected over the five year period in which Zackheim investigated the possible fates of Lieserl. Organized into nine subseries.
