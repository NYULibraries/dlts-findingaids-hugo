Abstract

Neil F. Polen (1945-1997) was a New York City bookseller and photographer. The Neil Polen Photographs include contact sheets, negatives, and prints by Polen dating from 1978 to 1991. The majority of the photographs in the collection were taken in the downtown neighborhoods of New York City, with some photographs in Albany, N.Y., and Haiti. In addition to individual portraits, the photographs depict architecture and cityscapes in New York City.

Biographical / Historical

Neil F. Polen (1945-1997) was a New York City bookseller and photographer. Polen was born in Albany, N.Y. on January 1, 1945. Between January 11, 1962 and March 5, 1964 he was a Seaman in the United States Navy. Polen died in Miami, FL on May 25, 1997. He is buried in Mishkan Tefilah Cemetery in West Roxbury, MA.

Scope and Content

The Neil Polen Photographs include contact sheets, negatives, and prints by Polen dating from 1978 to 1991.

The collection primarily contains photographs of downtown New York City neighborhoods, with a specific focus on the Chelsea neighborhood. These photographs include candid and posed portraits, cityscapes, architecture, grafitti, and signage. There are also photographs of locales outside of New York City, including Albany, N.Y., and Haiti.

Series 1 consists of contact sheets and negatives. Included are original envelopes which may have information on the individuals photographed--names or general descriptions--as well as the location of the photograph. Contact sheets also bear notations by Polen about editorial changes to the images. One set of contact sheets depicts Andy Warhol shortly before his death.

Series 2 includes a selection of prints from images in Series 1. While most prints are not accompanied by any identifying information, some have notations and inscriptions on the verso.

The collection does not include any biographical information about Polen, literature by or about him, or information about gallery showings of his work.
