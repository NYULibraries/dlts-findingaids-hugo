Abstract

This collection consists of personal letters from Irish author and editor Frank Harris to Constance Ruth Bonsall in 1916, at the time an aspiring young English writer.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Robert Kinloch in November 2018. The accession number associated with this donation is 2018.126.

Biographical Note

Frank Harris (born James Thomas Harris, 1856–1931) was an Irish editor, author, and journalist famous for his sexually explicit memoir My Life and Loves, as well as for his friendships with notable public and literary figures including Oscar Wilde, George Bernard Shaw, and Aleister Crowley. Harris was editor of London publications theEvening News, theFortnightly Review,The Saturday Review (for which he hired George Bernard Shaw), and the U.S. edition ofPearson's Magazine. In addition toMy Life and Loves, his work includesOscar Wilde: His Life and Confessions, Life of Bernard Shaw, andContemporary Portraits.

Constance Ruth Bonsall (1898-1994) was an aspiring English writer and the youngest daughter of a Devonshire vicar. She wrote in the 1920s using the pseudonym Charlotte Apple, and later published three novels under her maiden name (Ruth Bonsall) and her married name (Ruth Kinloch).
