Biographical Note

Nelson Sullivan (1948-1989) was a video artist who documented downtown New York City in the 1980s. Originally from South Carolina, Sullivan moved to Manhattan in the 1970s and began filming the city's art world and gay scene. He captured John Sex, Sylvia Miles, RuPaul, and other performers at downtown bars and nightclubs including Danceteria, the Limelight, Palladium, and the Pyramid Club. Sullivan also chronicled house parties at his apartment at 5 Ninth Avenue in the Meatpacking District, gallery openings, park and street festivals, conversations with taxi drivers, and the ruminations of his friends including fashion designer Alexis Del Lago and journalist Michael Musto on film. He recorded almost 2,000 hours of tape, at first using a VHS-loading camera and later an 8mm video camera. Sullivan's filmmaking is recognizable for its point of view transitions and its stream-of-consciousness narration. He also often pointed a wide-angle lens at his own face while filming to record his commentary on the people, performances, or events that he captured. Sullivan made films up until his death from a heart attack on July 4, 1989.

Scope and Content

The Nelson Sullivan Video Collection is a collection of videorecordings made by video artist Nelson Sullivan (1948-1989). The collection consists of videotapes, film, photographic material and objects related to the city's art world and LGBT community as well as performances in clubs, house parties, and interactions with Sullivan's friends. The videos cover the period 1976-1989.

The collection is comprised of four series:

Series I: Videorecordings contains 8 boxes of videorecordings made by Nelson Sullivan.

Series II: Film (currently unavailable)

Series III: Photographic Material contains 1 binder of slides covering the period between 1975 and 1977.

Series IV: Objects (currently unavailable)

Arrangement

The archivist preserved the original order of the video collection as donated by the estate of Nelson Sullivan. The series of videorecordings have not been chronologically or thematically rearranged.

Provenance

The Nelson Sullivan Video Collection was donated to Fales Library by the estate of Nelson Sullivan, overseen by Dick Richards, David Goldman, and Robert Coddington operating collectively as the 5 Ninth Avenue Project, in September 2012.
