Historical Note

The Scott and Gary Show was a cable access variety show that ran from 1983 to 1989 and featured punk and indie bands, live audience dance parties, and comedy skits. Created and hosted by Scott Lewis and Gary Winter, the show was filmed in New York City from 1983 to 1986 and, at the invitation of cable access producer Jeff Krulik, in the Washington DC metro area, from 1986 to 1989.

The show is notable for early appearances of bands such as the Butthole Surfers and the Beastie Boys (who appear with drummer Kate Schellenbach, later of Luscious Jackson). Other musical guests include the Ben Vaughn Combo, Raunch Hands, Half Japanese, Shockabilly, and Velvet Monkeys.

Scope and Content note

The Scott and Gary Show Archive comprises materials related to the cable-access show, produced and hosted by Scott Lewis and Gary Winter, that ran from 1983 to 1989. Materials include videotapes of the show; photocopies of a chronology of the series, episode descriptions, correspondence, clippings, and photographs; and videotapes of Jeff Krulik's "A Tribute to the Scott and Gary Show", their appearance on the CBS-TV sitcom "Kate and Allie", and an in-depth video interview with the show's creators.

The collection is arranged into two series:

Series I: Informational Binders. This series contains photocopies of a chronology of the series, episode descriptions, correspondence, clippings, and photographs.

Series II: Video. This series is divided into two subseries. Subseries A contains access copies for the researcher. At present, access copies have not been created for all material. Please contact a Fales archivist. Subseries B contains original video elements.
