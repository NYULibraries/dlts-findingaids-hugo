Scope and Contents

Series I: Research Files contains Yamamoto's research materials on shiatsu, macrobiotics, yoga, and alternative, non-Western healing methods. Research files consist of notebooks, conference and workshop packets, printed material, recipes, clippings, audio and video recordings. The audio and video recordings consist of commercial and non-commercial audiocassette tapes, CDs, DVDs, and VHS tapes. The content of most of the audiovisual materials pertains to prominent figures within the macrobiotic and alternative medicine community such as Michio Kushi, George and Lima Ohsawa, and Louise Hay. The content of several of the VHS tapes is unknown due to lack of labels. Some of the materials in Series I are in Japanese. Materials date from 1965 to 2010.

Arrangement

The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor. Paper materials are listed first, followed by audio and video recordings, and two objects are listed at the end.

Series II: Books and Magazines, 1968-2008

Extent

13 Linear Feet in 12 record cartons and 1 flat box.

Scope and Contents

Series II: Books and Magazines contains publications from Yamamoto's library pertaining to shiatsu, macrobiotic living and cooking, yoga, nutrition, acupuncture, and alternative non-Western healing methods. Some of the books are authored by Yamamoto herself, including Barefoot Shiatsu,The Shiatsu Handbook, andWhole Health Shiatsu. Other book authors prominently represented within the series include Edward Esko, Wendy Esko, Louise Hay, Alex Jack, Aveline Kushi, Michio Kushi, Patrick McCarty, George Ohsawa, and Lima Ohsawa. The most common magazine titles within the series are Amberwaves, AOBTA Pulse (American Organization for Bodywork Therapies of Asia), Christina Cooks, Compas, Healthways, Ignoramus, Macrobiotics Today, Macrobiotique, and Vegetarian Times. Some of the books and magazines are in Japanese. Materials date from 1968 to 2008.

Arrangement

The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Series IV: Patient Files, 1977-2002

Extent

3.7 Linear Feet in 6 card boxes and 1 manuscript box.

Scope and Contents

Series IV: Patient Files contains the files Yamamoto created for patients she consulted and treated for various ailments. The files consist of index cards with patients' contact information and reason(s) for consultation. The materials contain sensitive data and researchers wishing to view these materials should contact fales.library@nyu.edu for more information. Materials date from 1977 to 2002.

Arrangement

The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Conditions Governing Access
