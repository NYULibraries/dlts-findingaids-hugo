Abstract

Newman Levy (1888-1966) was a lawyer and author. The Newman Levy Papers inlcude manuscripts of his articles, book reviews, drama, fiction, verse, and materials related to an unpublished biography of Franklin P. Adams

Provenance

The Newman Levy Papers were donated to NYU by The Players Club in December 1991.

Biographical Note

Newman Levy was born in 1888. His father was the well-known New York criminal lawyer Abraham Levy. Newman Levy graduated from New York University Law school in 1911, and practiced law until 1961. He was an assistant district attorney for New York County from 1916 to 1919. Levy also wrote light verse and skits for Broadway revues. His books inlcude Gay but Wistful and the autobiography My Double Life: Adventures in Law and Letters. He died in New York in 1966.

Scope and Content

The collection consists of 7 boxes of manuscript material of articles, book reviews, drama, fiction, verse, and an unpublished biography of Franklin P. Adams, including some letters written to Levy by people who knew Adams.
