Abstract

The Gathering of the Tribes Archive includes correspondence, manuscripts, ephemera, photographic material, business and administrative records, video, audio, legal and financial records related to the operations of A Gathering of the Tribes, a literary magazine, gallery and performance space in New York's East Village.

Provenance

The Gathering of the Tribes Archive was purchased by NYU's Fales Library and Special Collections from Steve Cannon in 2007.

Historical Note

A Gathering of the Tribes is a literary magazine, gallery and performance space in New York's East Village. Founded in 1991 by Steve Cannon in his apartment, Tribes began as an informal salon for cultural exchange between poets, artists, musicians and performers. The collaborative, explorative work done in the space culminated in the founding of the Gathering of the Tribes magazine, whose first issue was published in the autumn of 1991. In 1993, part of the space became the Tribes Gallery, which plays host to exhibitions, readings, and performances.

Since its inception, A Gathering of the Tribes has sought to engage with and promote work from multi-cultural, interdisciplinary, and underrepresented writers and artists. Those featured at the gallery or published in the magazine include Ishmael Reed, Wynton Marsalis, Anyssa Kim, David Hammons, and Dora Espinoza. Tribes also publishes work under its Fly By Night Press imprint. Still in operation, Tribes remains a vibrant contributor to the East Village literary, artistic and performance scene alongside such venues as Nuyorican Poets Cafe and the Living Theatre.

Scope and Content

The Gathering of the Tribes Archive contains correspondence, manuscripts, ephemera, photographic material, business and administrative records, video, audio, legal and financial records. Materials in the archive document the operations of A Gathering of the Tribes, a literary magazine, gallery and performance space, and its publishing imprint, Fly By Night Press. The collection is arranged into seven series:

Series I: Media contains the audiovisual portion of the collection, inclusing video and audio cassettes.

Series II: Scrapbooks contains binders of material compiled by Tribes employees related to the gallery space, including ephemera, photographic material and event documentation.

Series III: Project Files contains files related to events, publications, and exhibitions, including correspondence, ephemera, artists' portfolios, and manuscripts.

Series IV: Grant Applications contains the records of all grant applications drafted by A Gathering of the Tribes.

Series VI: Publications contains printed material either published by, or associated with, A Gathering of the Tribes and Fly By Night Press.

Series VII: Data Storage contains electronic media, including compact discs and floppy discs.

Arrangement

The archive was arranged into six series based on the organization of items upon their arrival to the library. Archival materials were arranged at the folder level, alphabetically by folder title. Folder contents remain in the order in which they arrived at the library. Wherever possible, original folders were retained, numbered and described. Some folders were replaced due to preservation concerns. Materials that were too large for their original folders were rehoused in oversize boxes or into a map-case drawer. Audiovisual and media items were described at the item level. Scrapbooks were rehoused in archival binders for safe storage and access.

Physical Characteristics and Technical Requirements

Access to audiovisual materials in this collection is available through digitized access copies. Researchers may view an item's original container, but the media themselves are not available for playback because of preservation concerns. Materials that have already been digitized are noted in the collection's finding aid and can be requested in our reading room. To request an access copy, or if you are unsure if an item has been digitized, please contact special.collections@nyu.edu with the collection name, collection number, and a description of the item(s) requested. A staff member will respond to you with further information.

Separated Materials

Four issues of A Gathering of the Tribes were removed from the archive to be catalogued. They can be found in the Fales Library's Downtown Collection, via Bobcat.
