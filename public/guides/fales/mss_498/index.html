Abstract

New York-based curator, art consultant, and art historian Richard D. Marshall (1947-2014) worked for 20 years at the Whitney Museum, served as art editor of the Paris Review, and was curator at the Lever House Art Collection in New York. This collection contains erotic pulp novels; gay erotic and pornographic magazines, zines, serials, and internet fiction stories; widely-themed calendars and catalogs; gay erotic and pornographic illustrations; and pornographic films in VHS and DVD format from the 1980s to the early 2000s. Collectible dolls and other related items can also be found in this collection.

Biographical Note

New York-based curator, art consultant, and art historian Richard D. Marshall (1947-2014) worked for 20 years at the Whitney Museum, where he organized numerous Biennial Exhibitions of contemporary art, and many seminal one-artist and group exhibitions. He served as art editor of the Paris Review, from 1975-1990 and was also curator for more than a decade at the Lever House Art Collection in New York beginning in 2003. He passed away in California in 2014 at the age of 67.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains erotic pulp novels from the 1970s to the 1990s; gay erotic and pornographic magazines, zines, and serials spanning the 1940s through 2011; themed calendars and catalogs; gay erotic and pornographic illustrations; pornographic internet fiction stories written during the 1980s through the 2000s; and pornographic films in VHS and DVD format from the 1980s to the early 2000s. Tom of Finland "001 Rebel" and "Carlos the First Out and Proud Boyfriend" collectible dolls, as well as other related items can also be found in this collection. This collection illustrates the accumulation of pornography and erotic material by a gay man with a wide range of sexual interests, while living in New York during the 1970s through the 2000s. Additionally these same materials were used by Marshall as research and content for his proposed book illustrating homoerotic art throughout history. This collection includes a mock-up of the proposed book with supporting material.

Processing Information

This collection was processed and an inventory spreadsheet was made by Fales Library student workers between 2016 and 2017. The material was kept in the original order as it was found in the donor's office. In 2017, an archivist wrote a finding aid and ingested the spreadsheet into a folder level inventory.

Arrangement

This collection was kept in the creator's original arrangement, grouped by item type. Within these groupings, items were arranged and series were made by Fales student workers.
