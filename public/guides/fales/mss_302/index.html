Abstract

Allison Wolfe (b. 1969), a songwriter and arts journalist, co-founded the third wave feminist punk movement, Riot Grrrl, and the feminist punk fanzine, "Girl Germs." She sang in several bands throughout the 1990s and 2000s including Bratmobile and Cold Cold Hearts, and organized Ladyfest, a non-profit feminist music festival, in 1999. Materials in this collection include two dresses, three "performance slips," and one pair of shoes.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Allison Wolfe in 2010. The accession number associated with this gift is 2010.302.

Biographical Note

Allison Wolfe was born in Memphis Tennessee in 1969 and grew up in an all-female household. Her mother, a lesbian feminist, started the first women's health clinic in Olympia, Washington. Wolfe is a songwriter and arts journalist who co-founded the third wave feminist punk movement, Riot Grrrl. She also co-founded the feminist punk fanzine, "Girl Germs," and sang in several bands throughout the 1990s and 2000s including Bratmobile, Cold Cold Hearts, Deep Lust, Partyline, Cool Moms, and Sex Stains. In 1999 she organized Ladyfest, a non-profit feminist music festival. Wolfe received a master's degree in arts journalism from the University of Southern California's Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism.

Scope and Contents

This collection consists of two dresses, three "performance slips," and one pair of shoes. Wolfe wore the slips during the 1990s while performing with the band, Bratmobile. The dresses and shoes are consistent with the style of clothing women wore in the 1990s and early 2000s, which is associated with the third wave feminist punk movement.
