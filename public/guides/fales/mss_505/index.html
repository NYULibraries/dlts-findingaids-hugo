Biographical Note

Kenneth Eugene Silverman (1936-2017) was a Lithuanian-American magician, biographer and New York University professor who specialized in Colonial American literature. After completing his PhD in English at Columbia University in 1964, Silverman taught English at New York University until his retirement in 2001. Silverman was the co-director of NYU's American Civilization department and writer of several biographies such as Edgar A. Poe: Mournful and Never-Ending Remembrance and The Life and Times of Cotton Mather for which he won a Pulitzer Prize for Biography and a Bancroft Prize in American history in 1985. The Kenneth Silverman Papers mainly consists of photocopies of Silverman's research findings, detailed notes and annotations for his 1991 biography, Edgar A Poe: Mournful and Never-Ending Remembrance.

Scope and Contents

This collection mainly consists of photocopies of Silverman's research findings, detailed notes and annotations for his 1991 biography, Edgar A Poe: Mournful and Never-Ending Remembrance. The photocopied research materials give an overview of Edgar Allan Poe's personal and professional life ranging from his childhood, family history and romantic relationships with alleged fiance Sarah Elmira Royster and wife Virginia Poe. They consist of correspondence and journal entries from various manuscript and archival collections across the United States, maps of Poe's homes in various cities such as Baltimore and Philadelphia, newspaper articles and book extractions from about 1800 to the late 1890s. Silverman was also well known for his research intensive writing process. The Kenneth Silverman Papers is a reflection of this process.

Processing Information

Materials in this collection were placed in new acid-free folders and boxes. Original caption information, was transcribed onto archival folders. The collection was arranged into two series based on the original order of the records and original folder titles were retained.

Arrangement

This collection has been arranged into two series reflecting the different types of documents in the collection, Silverman's research photocopies and Silverman's research notes. The two series are Series I: Journal of Research Citations and Photocopies of Reasearch Files and Series II: Research Notes and Annotations.
