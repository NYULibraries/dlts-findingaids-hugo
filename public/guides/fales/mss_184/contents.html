Series I: Administrative records

Scope and Contents note

This series contains administrative records pertaining to the operation and maintenance of the Gallery. The series includes correspondence, general administrative files (including gallery bylaws and artist contracts), minutes from meetings, membership information, and programming and curatorial information. In addition, this series contains publications of the Gallery, press and writings about A.I.R., artist print portfolios, and assorted documents pertaining to activism, such as protest letters written to other museums.

Series II: Individual artists (see also Oversize)

Scope and Contents note

This series contains files pertaining to individual artists who have been represented by the A.I.R. Gallery, such as catalogs, correspondence, and newspaper or magazine articles about the artist. NB: the contents of this series does not comprise a complete list of every artist who has exhibited at A.I.R.. The date that corresponds to each item represents the year the artist joined the gallery, in accordance with the original folder designation. If no such designation existed on the original folder, the date is listed as "n.d." For more information regarding a particular artist not represented here, please contact the A.I.R. Gallery.

This series contains photographs and slides of A.I.R. exhibitions or of artworks by individual artists. The structure of the subseries remains faithful to the A.I.R. photo and slide inventory as it arrived at the Fales Library. The numbering system used by A.I.R. has been kept intact and is noted in the finding aid below. Subseries U consists of photographs and slides that were not part of the initial photos/slides series, but were received later as ancillary material. Subseries V consists of the A.I.R. Slide Registry, which was kept onsite at the Gallery as a resource for curators and other artists. The names listed in the finding aid for each binder does not comprise a complete list of the contents of that binder.

Some folders in the SLIDE subseries contain no slides, reflecting that slide sleeves were empty when collection was accessioned at Fales.
