Abstract

This collection consists of documents concerning slavery in Cuba between 1809 and 1898. Materials include telegrams, contracts, identification papers, travel licenses, marriage and baptism records, death and burial certificates, purchase and insurance documents, and manumission records documenting various aspects of slavery in Cuba, including the economics of the Atlantic slave trade, demographics of Cuban populations, England's abolition of slavery, the transportation of Chinese workers to Cuba, family relationships in the context of slavery, living and working conditions on sugar cane plantations, and manumission processes.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials in this collection were purchased from a bookseller in 2017; original provenance is unknown. The accession number associated with this purchase is 2019.010.

Processing Information

Documents with brittle paper were conserved to prevent immediate loss and documents with ink damage were housed in mylar with paper backing for additional support. Materials were described on the collection-level. The folder-level inventory was created by the collection's seller and modified by an archivist to remove deragatory terms and contextual details not relevant to the items.

Arrangement

This collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the seller.

Separated Materials

Folder 1 in the inventory, a grouping of 80 documents concerning a case of five slaves at a sugar mill who killed a man they believed to be the Governor General of Cuba, was removed from the collection and cataloged separately as HV6535.C9 C66 1842.
