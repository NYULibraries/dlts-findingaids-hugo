Biographical Note

The Tommy Turner Film Collection encompass the filmmaker's original film elements for "Where Evil Dwells" (1985, created in collaboration with Daid Wojnarowicz), as well as prints of "Simonland "(1984, created in collaboration with Richard Kern) and "Rat Trap" (1986, created in collaboration with Tessa Hughes-Freeland).

Turner was in possession of most of the materials created for "Where Evil Dwells." Unfortunately, much of the footage was lost in a fire at Turner's apartment before the film was completed. What remains is included in this collection. Among the reels is an "epic trailer" version of the film that Turner and Wojnarowicz put together. Other reels include raw footage for the project.

Processing Information

Tommy Turner's Simonland (1984) and Rat Trap (1985), his collaboration with Tessa Hughes-Freeland, were preserved in 2015 thanks to the National Film Preservation Foundations Avante-Garde Masters award.

Provenance

Films donated by Tommy Turner to Fales Library in October 2014, after being on deposit since 1999.

Audiovisual Access Policies and Procedures

Access to audiovisual materials in this collection is available through digitized access copies. Researchers may view an item's original container, but the media themselves are not available for playback because of preservation concerns. Materials that have already been digitized are noted in the collection's finding aid and can be requested in our reading room.
