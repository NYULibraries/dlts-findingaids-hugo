Abstract

The Godzilla Asian American Arts Network Archive houses the materials of the Godzilla Asian American Arts Network. The collection includes correspondence, exhibition dossiers, administrative materials, publications, and related materials.

Provenance

Ken Chu, founder of the Godzilla: Asian American Arts Network, donated the materials to the Fales Library in 2005.

Historical Note

Godzilla was an Asian American arts collective and support network founded in 1990. Godzilla's founding members believed that Asian American artists did not have a suitable organization to support, promote, and encourage their visual arts, and Godzilla sought to fill this need. Godzilla disbanded in 2001.

Scope and Content Note

The Godzilla Asian American Arts Network Archive contains documents relating to the activities and goals of the Godzilla Asian American Arts Network. Included are documents detailing the administrative and artistic aspects of Godzilla and related arts organizations.

Arrangement

The Godzilla Asian American Arts Network Archive has been arranged into five series: Correspondence, Exhibitions, Newsletters and Publicity, Administrative Materials, and Related Organizations.

Where applicable, the folders within the five series have been arranged chronologically. The contents of the folders are also arranged by date unless otherwise organized by Godzilla.
