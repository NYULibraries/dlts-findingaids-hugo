Series I: Play Scripts and Production Records, 1961-2001

Extent

8.5 Linear Feet in 16.5 manuscript boxes and 13 computer files.

Scope and Content

This series includes original and revised scripts in both paper and electronic format for a little over half of Irene Fornés' work, including some of her earliest plays and her last working play, Rue de Fleurus (concerning Gertrude Stein). It also includes a script for Minuski, a previously-unknown work, and La Plaza Chica, Fornés' translation and adaptation of her play Abingdon Square into Spanish. In addition, there are scores for some of her plays with songs; a libretto for an opera; and scripts and staging notes for Fornés' directing, translating, and adapting of other writers' plays or stories (such as Henrik Ibsen's Hedda Gabler and Anton Chekhov's Uncle Vanya and Drowning). There are numerous drafts or versions of Fornes' scripts written, edited, or adapted for different productions of a play, or revised after the completion of a run of a particular production. Many of the scripts are not complete, and most of them have Fornés' handwritten--often extensive--annotations and revisions. Production records for various stagings of her own plays and productions she directed of others' works include memos, notes, set designs, cast lists, and calls for auditions.

Arrangement

This series was divided into two subseries to distinguish between Fornés' work as a writer and director of her own plays and her work directing and translating plays of other authors. Folders in both subseries are arranged in alphabetical order by play title. For each title, folders are arranged by genre or document type. In cases in which plays are known by different titles, the best-known title is listed first, followed by the alternate title. Some folders may contain material for multiple titles.

Series II: Notes, Scenarios, and Writings in Process, 1960s-1990s, undated

Extent

2.75 Linear Feet in 4.75 manuscript boxes.

Scope and Content

This series contains diverse writings and drawings on paper recording Fornés' internal artistic processes and ideas. It includes notes, scenarios, stories and character studies; dialogs, monologues and individual lines; ideas for plays, films and dances; journal and diary entries; fragments of narratives and numerous other types of writings in process; and a small amount of correspondence. Some of the writings are typed, and many are handwritten. Formats range from notebooks to the backs of envelopes and smaller scraps. Almost none of the items are dated, with the exception of a few materials from the 1980s to the 1990s. Most of the materials originally received in labeled folders retain their original folder titles (these are enclosed by quotation marks); the remaining materials have been placed in folders labeled "miscellaneous short notes."

Series V: Family Papers, 1947-2000

Extent

0.1 Linear Feet in 2 folders.

Scope and Content

This series contains government documents and applications for government assistance, and documents and correspondence concerning emigration from Cuba and immigration to the United States, including Irene Fornés' naturalization certificate. Also included are affidavits and requests by Fornés and members of her extended family to Cuban and U.S. authorities to facilitate emigration of relatives in Cuba to the United States. In addition, there are applications by Fornés' mother, Carmen Collado Fornés for public housing and her food stamp identification cards. Nongovernmental official documents such as a travel identification card for American Youth Hostels, Inc. are also part of the series.

Series VII: Teaching Materials and Students' Work, 1971-2005

Extent

1.25 Linear Feet in 3 manuscript boxes.

Scope and Content

This series consists of students' handwritten, typewritten, or mimeographed scenarios; scripts for plays and playlets; stories; and poems. In addition, the series includes flyers, correspondence, exercise instructions, participant lists, play notes, and drawings that pertain to, or that arose from, Irene Fornés' teaching and playwriting workshops. She briefly taught elementary school students, but the bulk of her students were college students and adults who were emerging playwrights. Because Fornés' method for teaching plays included close collaboration, a number of these scripts were co-written by several different students as well as with Fornés herself (Meche is one clear example). In addition to those who received formal instruction from Fornés through workshops, lectures or classes, the series also includes materials from informal "students" of hers who may never have been enrolled in any of the courses she taught--aspiring playwrights whom she seems to have mentored or for whose work she seems to have provided criticism.

Series VIII: Subject/Research Files, 1919-2005

Extent

3.5 Linear Feet in 7 manuscript boxes and 1 computer file.

Scope and Content

This series includes a wide-ranging collection of materials gathered as research for Fornés' plays on subjects as varied as fortune-telling, President John F. Kennedy's assassination, Fidel Castro, and silent films, as well as articles documenting Fornés' career and the reception of her plays, her interests in drama and art, and her extensive travels, as well as those of her mother.
