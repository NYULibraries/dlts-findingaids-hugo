Abstract

Founded in 2001, the Commercial Closet Association, renamed AdRespect, began as a way to track and document lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) themed advertisements globally. Michael Wilke, the founding executive director of the Commercial Closet Association, has researched and written about LGBT issues in advertising for over twenty years. The collection is comprised of two series: print and media publications and photographs. The print and media publications' series consists of mostly earmarked magazines and newspaper articles that were a part of Michael Wilke and the Commercial Closet Association's research ranging from 1975-2017. The photograph series is comprised of Wilke's personal photographs of family reunions, vacations and LGBT community events.

Biographical / Historical

Michael Wilke has researched and documented the marketing of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) themed advertisements as a business journalist and nonprofit leader since 1992. Wilke is the founding executive director of the Commercial Closet Association, which began in New York City in May 2001. The Commercial Closet Association, now renamed AdRespect, continues its mission to promote "advertising that respects diversity, gender identity/expression, and sexual orientation for a more accepting society and better business results." Wilke studied journalism at the School of Visual Arts in New York City, and earned a masters of marketing at Zicklin Business School of Baruch College-City University of New York. Previously, he worked as a business reporter at Advertising Age and has written about LGBT advertising and media for The New York Times, Adweek, Brandweek, The Advocate, Haymarket's Revolution marketing magazine, and Inside Media. He received the Gay and Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation (GLAAD) Media Award and was one of OUT magazine's OUT 100 in 2001. Wilke has served as an advertising judge for the ANA Multicultural Excellence Awards, a Crain Lecturer at Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism, and was a Hearst Professional in Residence fellowship at the University of Colorado at Boulder.

Scope and Contents

The collection documents the efforts of the Commercial Closet Association to collect and analyze domestic and international print and media advertisements that engage the LGBT community in positive and negative ways. The collection is comprised of two series: print and media publications and photographs. The print and media publications' series consists of mostly earmarked magazines and newspaper articles that were a part of Michael Wilke and the Commercial Closet Association's research ranging from 1975-2017. Major publications included within this series include Pride, Passport, Out, Outtraveler, Attitude, The Advocate, Genre, and The New York Times. The photograph series is comprised of Wilke's personal photographs of family reunions, vacations and LGBT community events.

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning materials were placed in new acid-free folders and boxes. Original caption information, when available, was transcribed onto folders. Print and media publication titles and dates were written onto folders. Michael Wilke's photographs were grouped and accompanied with index card descriptions which the archivist transcribed onto the folders.

Arrangement

Organized into two series: Series I: Print and Media Publications; and Series II: Photographs. The materials in Series I are arranged chronologically and by publication title. The materials in Series II are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.
