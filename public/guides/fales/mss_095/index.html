Biographical / Historical

Novelist Erich Maria Remarque was born on June 22, 1898 in Osnabrück, Germany. He spent his childhood in imperial Osnabrück, and later served in the German army in World War I on the Western Front. Remarque's books were burned and banned in Nazi Germany, and he lived in exile in Switzerland and the United States following the revocation of his German citizenship in 1938. Remarque married, divorced, and remarried Ilse Jutta Zambona. In 1958 he married American actress Paulette Goddard, and they remained married until his death.

Remarque is best known for his novel All Quiet On the Western Front, which was first published in 1929. His other published novels include The Road Back (1931), Three Comrades (1936, 38), Flotsam (1941), Arch of Triumph (1945), The Black Obelisk (1956), Heaven Has No Favorites (1961), and The Night in Lisbon (1962). Many of these works were adapted into films, often shortly after their publication. According to the Erich Maria Remarque-Peace Center, Remarque critically examined German history in his works, upholding "the preservation of human dignity and humanity in times of oppression, terror and war." Remarque died on September 25, 1970 in Locarno, Switzerland.

Scope and Content

The Erich Remarque Papers Accretion consists of correspondence, notes and contracts primarily dealing with the financial and legal aspects of Remarque's life and works. It also includes ephemera and some audiovisual recordings documenting adaptations of works by Remarque; a small selection of typed short story mauscripts with handwritten notes; some personal correspondence and photographs of Paulette Goddard; correspondence, photos, financial statements and auction catalogs related to the Remarques collections of art, textiles, jewelry and other decorative arts; as well as a small amount of photographs that appear to show city and country scenes in Germany.

This guide describes an accretion to the Erich Maria Remarque Papers. An additional 63 boxes are detailed in a German language inventory available from the Fales Library. Please contact fales.library@nyu.edu for further assistance.

Arrangement

The Erich Maria Remarque Papers Accretion is organized into the following nine series:

Series I: Literary properties consists of correspondence, memos, contracts, agreements, drafts, royalty statements and notes related to the financial and legal aspects of novels, plays, short stories, articles and screenplays by Erich Maria Remarque, as well as related adaptations. It is organized into subseries arranged alphabetically by work title. Within each subseries, materials are arranged chronologically, and photocopies are filed based on dates of the originals.

Series II: General financial and legal consists of correspondence, memos, tax returns and other financial and legal documents of Erich Remarque and Paulette Goddard not primarily related to Remarques' literary output. It is arranged chronologically.

Series III: Art, textile and jewelry collections consists of documents related to the Remarques' collections of art, textiles, jewelry and other decorative objects. Subseries A: Correspondence and financial documents contains correspondence with auction houses, as well as sales statements. Subseries B: Catalogs contains primarily auction catalogs, many with handwritten notes and sales statements, as well as one non-auction catalog. Unless otherwise stated, the catalogs are from Sotheby's auction house.

Series IV: Short story manuscripts consists of mostly typed manuscripts, many with handwritten notes. It is arranged alphabetically by story title.

Series V: Paulette Goddard personal materials consists of correspondence, engagement books and planners. The bulk of the correspondence is from Paulette Goddard to her mother, Alta Goddard, although it includes letters from Erich Maria Remarque and several other correspondents to Paulette Goddard.

Series VI: Eleonara Duse telegrams contains a small number of telegrams from actress Eleonara Duse to theater director Paul Lindau. They have been retained in original order.

Series VII: Photographs consists of photographs and glass plate negatives, and is separated into three subseries. Subseries A: Erich Remarque consists of photos that appear to date from the 1920s and 1930s and include images of cars, city scenes, and country roads with many pictures of an unidentified woman. Subseries B: Paulette Goddard consists primarily of film promotional stills, along with some personal shots. Subseries C: Art, furniture, jewelry, rugs consists of photos primarily of items belonging to the Remarques that appear to have been used in appraisals and sales.

Series VIII: Film contains one film that is currently unavailable to researchers.

Series IX: Audio and Video recordings consists of at least one interview with Erich Remarque, along with a number of unidentified cassettes. All audio and video recordings are currently unavailable to researchers.

Series X: Objects consists of a handbag and portrait locket belonging to Paulette Goddard

Provenance

The bulk of the materials were transferred between 1993 and 2000 from the offices of Richard Kay, who served as a legal and financial advisor to Paulette Goddard. Many of the documents appear to have previously come from the offices of Harriet Pilpel, Erich Remarque's attorney and friend.
