Series I: Correspondence

Scope and Contents

Series I: Correspondence pertains to Margaret Strickland's personal and professional life, including correspondence from her closest friends, family, and colleagues in the publishing industry. This series features over 146 letters (over 660 pages) from Strickland's sweetheart "Louis", in addition to letters from her husband Richard Hugh Worley while he was on active service with the British Expeditionary Force and Kings Royal Rifle Corps during World War I.

Arrangement

These materials are arranged alphabetically by the name of the individual in contact with Strickland or the subject in conversation. In the case that a record includes more than one date expression, the listing will begin with its earliest. If a record is physically undated, approximate ranges are provided. These records are noted "undated" and followed by a "circa" estimate determined by the range represented in the same series.

Series II: Manuscripts

Scope and Content

Series II: Manuscripts documents Strickland's professional writing in the form of original typescript drafts with handwritten editing notation of over sixty-two titles, separated into four different subseries: A: Biographical, B: Novels, C: Plays, and D: Short Fiction & Non-Fiction. The final subseries, D: Short Fiction & Non-Fiction features small collections of Strickland's published work. "Five Animal Short Stories" [Box 4, Folder 20] and "Twelve Uncanny Stories" [Box 6, Folder 2] are handmade portfolio booklets of Strickland's stories that were published in The Blue Magazine & The Red Magazine, The Violet Magazine, and The Yellow Magazine, which were part of a small British popular fiction pulp format separated by subject and printed by the Amalgamated Press between July 1922 and December 1929. This series also comprises drafts of writing in various genres including adult mystery and romance fiction, children's adventure and detective fiction, historical fiction and creative non-fiction, as well as tests about animal care, food recipes and home remedy instructions.

Arrangement

These materials are arranged alphabetically by title or subject, then chronologically if applicable. In the case that a record includes more than one date expression, the listing will begin with its earliest. If a record is physically undated, approximate ranges are provided. These records are noted "undated" and followed by a "circa" estimate determined by the range represented in the same series and or subseries.

Series III: "Illustrated Amateur Magazine"

Scope and Contents

Series III: "Illustrated Amateur Magazine" features early examples Strickland sisters' art and writing. This series includes twelve issues of the title, a handmade publication Margaret created with her sisters Agnes, Diana Mary, and their friends in Bromley, Kent [now London], from 1897 - 1899. Despite their adolescent origins, these issues of "Illustrated Amateur Magazine" feature an especially acute knowledge of advertising and the magazine industry of the period. Contents include articles about current culture trends that range from art, bicycles, music, theater, to prize reader activities in the form of competitions and puzzles, to refined artwork in the form of ink drawings and watercolor paintings.

Arrangement

These materials are arranged chronologically. In the case that a record includes more than one date expression, the listing will begin with its earliest. If a record is physically undated, approximate ranges are provided. These records are noted "undated" and followed by a "circa" estimate determined by the range represented in the same series and or subseries.
