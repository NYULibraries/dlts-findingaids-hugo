Historical Note

The Black Student Service Center (BSSC) at New York University (NYU) was established in 1973 "to enrich the cultural, social, and educational experiences of the Black student." A representative committee of undergraduate students of color from NYU's University Heights and Washington Square campuses submitted their proposal to NYU Chancellor Eleazer Bromberg via Roscoe C. Brown, Jr., director of NYU's Institute of Afro-American Affairs.

The BSSC constitution states its purpose: "to provide concerned students with facilities to promote educational, political, cultural, social, and career-oriented exchange on campus."

The BSSC sponsored many annual events at NYU, including Black History Month talks, Kwanzaa celebrations, jazz concerts, career days, education forums, fashion shows, and an annual awards dinner.

In 1985 the BSSC received the Chancellor's Service Award for Programming at NYU.

Content Description

This collection contains organizational records from the Black Student Service Center at New York University. Included are correspondence, bylaws and constitutions, committee minutes, expense receipts and budgets, membership lists, publicity materials, and event photographs.
