Abstract

The Department of Photography and Imaging at the NYU Tisch School of the Arts is a four-year BFA program focused on the making and understanding of images through an arts curriculum grounded in liberal arts education. Students and faculty in the department work in varied modes of analog and digital photo-based image making and multimedia. This collection consists of materials created and collected by the Department of Photography and Imaging at the NYU Tisch School of the Arts from the late 1980s through the 2010s, and includes catalogs, programs from exhibitions, and copies of ISO magazine. The collection also includes audio and video recordings in both analog and electronic format of the department's events, guest lectures, and panel sessions, as well as examples the department's film library of commercial releases.

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning materials were rehoused in archival boxes and folders, and described on the collection-level with a box-list inventory. Born-digital items were identified and inventoried, but have not yet been forensically imaged, analyzed, or described. New York University Libraries follow professional standards and best practices when imaging, ingesting, and processing born-digital material in order to maintain the integrity and authenticity of the content.

Arrangement

This collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.
