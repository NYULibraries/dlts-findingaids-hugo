The Investment Bankers Association of America, in cooperation with New York University, founded the Institute of International Finance in 1926 as a fact-finding body concerned with foreign economic and financial conditions. This collection contains a scrapbook of press clippings that mention the Institute of International Finance from between 1927 and 1939 and "The Bulletins of NYU's Institute of Finance, 1926-1970: A Review Article," reprinted from the Journal of Economic Literature.

Historical Note

The Investment Bankers Association of America, in cooperation with New York University, founded the Institute of International Finance in 1926 as a fact-finding body concerned with foreign economic and financial conditions. John T. Madden, the Dean of New York University's School of Commerce, Accounts, and Finance directed the Institute. The Institute published Bulletins, answered inquiries, developed a finance library, and published studies about foreign economic conditions.

Scope and Contents

This collection includes a scrapbook that contains clippings from various sources including news organizations, newspapers, and pamphlets, from between 1927 and 1939 that mention the Institute of International Finance. Each item is labeled with its source and date. "The Bulletins of NYU's Institute of Finance, 1926-1970: A Review Article," by Samuel Katz, reprinted from the Journal of Economic Literature, is in the same box as the scrapbook.
