Abstract

This collection documents the history of the College of Engineering as written by a New York University (NYU) faculty member. Bryans served both as Assistant Dean and Professor of Engineering Mechanics at the College of Engineering in his four-decade career at the University. The collection contains manuscripts of and photographs from two separate histories of the College written by Bryans.

Biography of William Bryans

Born in New York City, William R. Bryans (1884-1974) attended Manual Training High School in Brooklyn from 1895-1902 before attending New York University (NYU). He graduated NYU with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering in 1906 and achieved the degree of Mechanical Engineer in 1909. Less than a year after graduation, Bryans took his first job at NYU, where he continued to work for over four decades in many capacities, including as a Professor of Engineering Mechanics, the Secretary of Faculty, and Assistant Dean. In 1949 he was named Professor Emeritus and was granted an Honorary Doctorate in Engineering.

During his academic career Bryans published several papers on the mechanics of materials and engineering education. He also authored two books, Concise Mechanics of Materials and Mechanics of Internal Work in 1928 and Resistance of Materials in 1931.

Bryans was a member of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers, the Society for the Promotion of Engineering Education, the American Association for the Advancement of Science, the American Association of University Professors, Iota Alpha, and the NYU Faculty Club. During World War I, Bryans served as Assistant Director in charge of instruction for the Student Army Training Corps from 1917-1918.

Scope and Content Note

This collection documents the historical research of an NYU alumnus who went on to serve the University as both a professor and an administrator. The collection contains manuscripts for two works on the history of the College of Engineering as well as photographs that illustrate each manuscript.
