Abstract

The Fred B. Renwick Papers contain materials related to the career of Fred Blackwell Renwick, a faculty member of New York University's Leonard N. Stern Business School from 1965 to 2001. Collection materials date between 1966 and 2015 and include some biographical materials, files related to Renwick's research and writings, his coursework, some coursework of other Stern professors of finance, as well as one file of student work.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Evangelynne (Lynne) C. Mueller in mid-December 2016. The accession number associated with this gift is 2017.001.

Biographical Note

Fred B. Renwick was a faculty member of New York University's Leonard N. Stern Business School from 1965 to 2001. Prior to teaching, Renwick earned a Bachelor's Degree from Morehouse College, S.M. from Harvard, MBA and Ph.D. in Finance from the Stern School of Business. While at NYU, his research interests included equity price and market predictability; behavioral finance; investments and finance eleemosynary institutions and corporations; security valuation, financial strategy, and portfolio and asset management; and models and analysis of speculative markets, both domestic and worldwide. In addition to teaching, he was also an accomplished author of published monographs, research articles, and a textbook. In 2007, a few years after retiring, Renwick even received an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters degree from Morehouse College in 2007. Renwick passed away at his home in Manhattan on December 4, 2014, survived by his partner Lynne Mueller.

Scope and Contents

The Fred B. Renwick Papers contain materials that relate to the professional career of Fred Blackwell Renwick, a professor of finance who taught at New York University's Leonard N. Stern Business School from 1965 to 2001. Collection materials date from 1966 through 2015, with the bulk of the collection dating between 1982 and 2001. Materials include Renwick's 2015 New York Times obituary; resumes; documents about Renwick's honors, awards, and grants; conference materials; correspondence; as well as research files, some of which relate to Renwick's articles or his collaborative projects with other professors, including the chapters he wrote for A Bibliography of Investments, which he co-authored with Malek K. Lashgari, Associate Professor of Finance at the University of Hartford, in 1990. Renwick's research files are followed by a number of general course files consisting of handouts, reading packets, and exercises, which are arranged in the collection by topic. In addition to these general files, the collection also contains specific course files that are arranged in chronological order by term. Courses include Foundations of Finance / Introduction to Finance; Financial Markets and Investments; Equity Instruments and Markets: Analysis, Predictability, Appraisal; Security Analysis; Investment Principles and Analysis; and Portfolio Analysis. Also included in the Renwick Papers are similar course materials generated by other Stern faculty members (both paper and electronic), as well as a file of student coursework.

Conditions Governing Access

Administrative records and unpublished reports of New York University are closed for a period of 20 years from the date of their creation. Access to files spanning multiple years will be opened to researchers based on the date of the most recent materials. Board of Trustees records are closed for 35 years from the date of creation. Materials related to personnel, grievances, job and fellowship searches and applications, and all files that fall under the federal Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) are permanently restricted. Additional restrictions may apply to other materials in this collection. For questions regarding specific restrictions, please contact the University Archives.

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning, materials were rehoused in archival record cartons, and the original folders of the collection were maintained. Loose materials were placed in acid-free folders, and dates were transcribed onto each file, whether original or new. A hardcover NYU Stern 2007 alumni directory was removed from the collection and transferred to the New York University Archives' collection of alumni directories, and the born-digital materials present in the collection were analyzed, imaged, and described.

Arrangement

The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor. Rough categories of biographical materials, research and writing files, and teaching materials comprise the collection.
