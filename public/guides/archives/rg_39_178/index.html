Abstract

This collection contains issues from the student newspaper the Washington Square News (established as the Washington Square Daily News), dating from 1973 to 2017. This newspaper documents life at NYU, local and national news, campus living, and student clubs. The collection is extensive but not comprehensive, there may be some issues that were published but are not included in this collection. This collection includes issues in their original print format as well as microfilmed copies. Some issues are bound.

Historical Note

New York University has had many student newspapers with many different names since its founding. The Heights campus and the Washington Square campus had separate student newspapers until 1973 when the Heights campus was closed. The Washington Square News was first published in the fall of 1973, and was preceded by the Washington Square Journal. Originally titled the Washington Square Daily News, the newspaper shortened its title in the fall of 1974. The newspaper is still being published as of 2019.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains issues from the student newspaper the Washington Square News (established as the Washington Square Daily News), dating from 1973 to 2017. This newspaper documents life at NYU, local and national news, campus living, and student clubs. This collection also contains Washington Square News's arts and culture publication, The Village Angle Magazine, dating from 1983 to 1988. The collection is extensive but not comprehensive, there may be some issues that were published but are not included in this collection. This collection includes issues in their original print format as well as microfilmed copies. Some issues are bound.
