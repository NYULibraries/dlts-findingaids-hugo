Abstract

Ivan L. Bennett was Acting President of New York University (NYU) from 1979 to 1981. This collection contains 76 speeches and remarks he delivered during his tenure, along with related correspondence, memoranda, and drafts.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

This collection was transferred from the President's Office to the Archives by Judith Chazen Walsh at the request of Lynne Brown, Assistant to the President, on June 8, 1981.

Biographical Note

Ivan Loveridge Bennett, Jr., M.D., served as acting president of New York University (NYU) from 1979 to 1981, when John C. Sawhill left the position to serve as deputy secretary of the U.S. Department of Energy. Dr. Bennett was succeeded by John Brademas.

Dr. Bennett held numerous administrative positions at NYU before serving as acting president. On January 21, 1969, Bennett was appointed Vice-President for Health Affairs and Director of the NYU Medical Center. On May 8, 1970, he was appointed director of the School of Medicine and Director of the Post-Graduate Medical School. (Bennett was the first person to hold both positions simultaneously.) In 1970 he became Dean of the New York University School of Medicine; in 1973 he was appointed Provost of the New York University Medical Center; and in 1976 Bennett was named Executive Vice-President for Health Affairs. Bennett did not seek election as President of New York University in 1981, opting instead to return to the Medical Center.

Bennett's career as a medical researcher, professor, and administrator had been well established by the time he arrived at New York University. He was an Associate Professor of Internal Medicine at Yale University from 1952 to 1954. He then moved to Johns Hopkins University, where he served as an Associate Professor of Medicine until 1957, when he was made a full Professor. In 1958 Bennett was named the Baxley Professor of Pathology and the director of that department. Bennett took a leave of absence from Johns Hopkins University in 1966 to become the Deputy Director and, later, Acting Director of U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson's Office of Science and Technology.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains 76 speeches and series of remarks delivered by Ivan L. Bennett between 1979 and 1981, during his tenure as acting president of New York University. This collection also includes related materials, including correspondence, memoranda, and drafts. The speeches are arranged chronologically: Box 1 contains addresses and remarks delivered between March 1979 and June 1980 and Box 2 contains speeches and remarks presented from July 1980 and June 1981.
