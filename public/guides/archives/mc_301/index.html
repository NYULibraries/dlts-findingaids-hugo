Abstract

This collection contains student handbooks from schools within New York University dating from 1854 to 2009. The early handbooks cover the university in its entirety, but later are often school- and program-specific.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials found in collection; provenance is unknown for the bulk of materials. 1928-1928 edition of The Waverly is the gift of Mark A. Goldstein, 2018.

Historical Note

The earliest handbooks from the collection date from the 1850s and touch upon subjects such as student behavior and locations of university buildings. Later handbooks were published for specific schools and specific programs within these schools.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains student handbooks dating from 1854 to 2009 generated by New York University and its component schools. These handbooks gave students information on services provided by the school, resources for student life, and student activities at NYU. It also contains guidebooks for financial aid, dormitories, and conducting research.

Preferred Citation

Published citations should take the following form: Identification of item, date; New York University Archives Collection of Student Handbooks; MC 301; box number; folder number; New York University Archives, New York University Libraries.

Arrangement

Items are arranged at the folder level. The handbooks are arranged first by their Record Group (based on school or program) and then chronologically within that RG.
