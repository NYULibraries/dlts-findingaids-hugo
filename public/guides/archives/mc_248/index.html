Abstract

The William F. Anselm Collection of New York University Memorabilia contains ephemera and publications dating from William F. Anselm's tenure as a student at New York University (Engineering '36). As a student, Anselm was active in Greek life as a member of the Delta Phi fraternity and the Inter-Fraternity Council. Biographical Note

William F. Anselm was born in Staten Island in 1914. He studied Electrical Engineering at New York University, graduating with a Bachelor of Science degree in 1936. As a student, Anselm was active in Greek life as a member of the Delta Phi fraternity and the Inter-Fraternity Council. He was also a member of Scabbard and Blade, a military honor society, and the ROTC Rifle and Pistol Club. After graduating from NYU, he worked for General Electric.

Scope and Contents

The collection contains ephemera and publications dating from William F. Anselm's tenure as a student at New York University (Engineering '36). Most of the materials were created by NYU between 1932 and 1938. These materials include invitations and programs for events hosted by the university or groups associated with it, including the military honor society Scabbard and Blade, the ROTC program, and the Engineering Societies. Other materials include assorted issues of the New York University Medley, a student magazine, NYU football ticket stubs and programs, and a purple quill pen labeled "NYU." A few items in the collection were not created by NYU, most of which are programs from New York City theatrical productions that Anselm presumably attended.
