Series I. Correspondence

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents note

1.0 linear feet (2 boxes) arranged chronologically. The correspondence series, which contains incoming and outgoing letters, documents the early relationship between the U.S. Peace Corps and the Research Institute for the Study of Man (RISM). The bulk of material was generated between March - April of 1962, and relates specifically to the training curriculum for Peace Corps Volunteers (PCV). Notable correspondents include Seargent Shriver, director of the Peace Corps, the Honorable Norman W. Manley, Beryl Bailey, H.D. Huggins and other faculty members of the University College of the West Indies (UCWI). Letters establishing potential placement, recruitment of training personnel and forms letters to new volunteers are also included.

Series VII. Teaching Papers

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents note

Two linear feet (4 boxes), arranged chronologically. This series highlights the academic and technical training taught in the program. These training materials have been organized as five subseries: Subseries A. Peace Corps Orientation, Program Syllabi, and Area Studies, on the history, geography and culture of Jamaica, W.I., including the original typescript, A Language Guide toJamaica, written by Beryl Bailey. Note: Recorded versions of this guide can be found in Series XIV, Audio-visual Materials. The papers in Subseries B. Technical Studies, relate to studies in the industrial arts, vocational education, biological science, agriculture, library science, nursing, plumbing, auto mechanics and other trades. Subseries C. American Studies and World Affairs focused on communism and other newsworthy events during the 1960s. The papers in Subseries D. Health Training, mainly relate to public health education. Subseries E. Physical Education relates to physical fitness soccer, cricket and other sports activities.
