Abstract

The collection consists of publications (bulletins, catalogs, announcements, etc.) that outline the course of study at New York University and its component schools, dating from 1832 to 2017. The publications are a mix of bound and unbound materials, as well as electronic records in the PDF format. They typically include an overview of the school, degree requirements, lists of faculty and administrators, and course descriptions. Early publications sometimes include lists of students and degrees conferred.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Processing Information

This collection was assembled by the New York University Archives staff. It was collectively referred to as "Series 3: Bulletins, Catalogs, Announcements" through 2018. This designation may appear on boxes and folders in the current collection.

In May 2019 additional course bulletins were discovered in the University Archives and were integrated into this collection.

Arrangement

The collection is organized into series based on the New York University Archives's record group classification system. Titles for the record groups are based on the school or program's current official name (as of 2019). For example, School of Commerce bulletins are included as part of the Stern School of Business record group.
