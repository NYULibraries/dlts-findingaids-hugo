Abstract

The Records of the Office of the Associate Dean of the School of Education contain mostly correspondence and memoranda between various School department heads and faculty and the Office of the Associate Dean, usually represented either by Dr. Elliot Mininberg, or the Associate Dean himself, Dr. Louis Kleinman. The memoranda are frequently budgetary in nature, requesting money allocation for departmental needs. There are also a number of different School of Education publications representing the interests and goals of the School's various departments.

Biography of Dr. Louis Kleinman

Dr. Louis Kleinman graduated from NYU with a bachelor of Science in 1948. He received a Master of Arts degree from Harvard University in 1949 and returned to NYU to earn his doctorate in education, which he received in 1959. Prior to earning his doctorate, he joined the staff at NYU in 1952 as Director of Educational Placement Services. From 1955 to 1960 he served as director of the Camden division of Rutgers University's School of Education. Kleinman returned to NYU in 1960, becoming head of the Teacher Education Section of NYU's Experimental Teaching Center. In 1961 he became head of the School's Center for Field Research and School Services. Kleinman retained this position when, in 1967, he was named to the new post of Associate Dean for Research, Field and Administrative Services.

Scope and Content

The Records of the Office of the Associate Dean of the School of Education spans 1962 to 1970, with most materials falling between 1967 and 1969. It is divided into three series: Series I: Departmental Administrative & Budgetary Memoranda; Series II: General Administrative Records; Series III: School of Education Publications. Series I is arranged alphabetically by department and consists of general correspondence and requests for funding for various projects between department heads and faculty and the Office of the Associate Dean as represented by either Dr. Elliot Mininberg (Director of Administrative Services and Assistant to the Associate Dean) or Associate Dean Kleinman himself. Series II contains a lot of general memoranda including a good deal from the Department of Administration and Supervision. Also included are copies of form letters and requests (both paid and unpaid) for budget allocations for food services and flyer/leaflet/pamphlet publication. Series III contains more correspondence dealing specifically with School of Education publications as well as copies of many of the publications themselves, primarily from 1968 and 1969.

This collection will be of use to those interested in the history of NYU's School of Education as well as the development of educational programs in the 1960s and 70s. The collection is reflective of the diversity of options available to students interested pursuing a career in education during this era.

Access Restrictions

Institutional records of New York University are closed for a period of 20 years from the date of their creation (the date on which each document was written). Board of Trustees records are similarly closed for 35 years from the date of creation. The opening date for files spanning several years will be 20 years from the most recent date. Access will be given to material already 20 years old contained within a collection that is not yet open when such material can be isolated from the rest of the collection.

Materials related to personnel, faculty grievances, job searches and all files with information that falls under the University's Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) policy are permanently restricted.

Please contact the University Archivist with specific questions regarding restrictions.
