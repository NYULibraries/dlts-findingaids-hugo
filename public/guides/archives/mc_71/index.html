Abstract

Frederick Gregory Nichols (1878-1954) was a prominent business educator who sought to standardize, regulate, and promote business education in public schools. This collection contains a typescript copy of Frederick G. Nichols' memoirs related to his professional career and a memorandum regarding the manuscript's transfer to New York University Archives.

Biographical Note

Frederick Gregory Nichols (1878-1954) was a prominent business educator who sought to standardize, regulate, and promote business education in public schools. Nichols served as City Director of Business Education for Rochester, New York (1905-1909), State Director of Business Education for New York (1909-1911), and Federal Representative of Business Education. In 1934, Nichols helped establish the National Council for Business Education. Nichols was a professor of business education at Harvard University School of Education (1922-44); he retired as a Professor Emeritus.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains a typescript copy of Frederick G. Nichols' memoirs describing his professional life as an advocate for business education in public schools, and a memorandum that describes the transfer of the manuscript to New York University Archives.

Related Materials

Two other copies of Frederick G. Nichols' manuscript are held in different archives. A second copy typewritten manuscript is held at the UCLA Charles E. Young Research Library Department of Special Collections. A hand-written manuscript was given to the Delta Pi Epsilon National Office, in St. Peter, Minnesota in 1980.

In 1979, Nichols' memoirs were published by Delta Pi Epsilon as Frederick G. Nichols' Memoirs, 1878-1954: The Early View of Business Education. This publication is significantly shorter than the manuscript version.
