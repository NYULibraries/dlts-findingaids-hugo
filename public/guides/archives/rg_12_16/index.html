Abstract

The Center for Student Life Records contains paper and electronic records of student publications; fliers; event posters and calendars; photographs; brochures; guides for campus programs for incoming students; and programs from the Center for Student Life created for the students from 1958-2018. The materials of the collection respond to the history of student social life at New York University, which include student-led organizations, lecture series, as well as sororities and fraternities. Historical Note

The Center for Student Life engages New York University students during their transition into campus life. The Center serves as a central point of information and campus-wide events, and helps students navigate various aspects of the university, including clubs, fraternities, sororities, class activities boards, and community service opportunities.

Scope and Contents

The Center for Student Life Records contains paper and electronic records of student publications; fliers; event posters and calendars; photographs; brochures; guides for campus programs for incoming students; and programs from the Center for Student Life created for the students from 1958-2018. The materials of the collection respond to the history of student social life at New York University, which include student-led organizations, lecture series, as well as sororities and fraternities. The program guides document President's Service Award ceremonies, parent activities, and student orientation from 1989 to 2018. Additionally, the collection consists of original student artwork from certain members of these organizations. There are signed performance posters by reputable artists, such as musicians Sufjan Stevens and Joan Jett in addition to the comedian Mike Birbiglia.

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning, materials were placed in new acid-free folders and boxes, and described on the collection-level.

At the time of accessioning, materials from accession 2019.010 were placed in acid-free boxes. A portion of the collection's content were foldered, while other printed materials remain unfoldered, but are housed in appropriately sized acid-free containers. Original caption information, when available, was transcribed onto acid-free archival folders. The archivist maintained the original order of the Box Content List, but certain materials were consolidated and include a Materials Separation Sheet that recorded these transfers.

Electronic records on CD-R format have been identified, inventoried, and physically removed from their original boxes but have not yet been imaged or analyzed. New York University Libraries follows professional standards and best practices when imaging, ingesting, and processing born-digital material in order to maintain the integrity of the content.

Arrangement

This collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Custodial History

This collection was created and stored in the Center for Student Life. Various administrators and employees in the office collected the items. The majority of the items were collected and backed by Bob Butler, former director of the center for student life, but others may have also contributed to the collection as well.
