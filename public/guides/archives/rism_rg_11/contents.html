Series II. Ford Foundation Grant Papers, 1967 - 1971, undated

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents note

.5 linear feet (1 box), arranged chronologically. The papers in this series relate to conference funding and include expense sheets, correspondence, invoices, and reimbursement forms submitted by conference participants. Highlights include the Final report to the Ford Foundation, which provides a historical context and rationale for the conference. Box 2 containing Series II and part of Series III were returned to RISM March 2009.

Series III. Conference Papers: Research and Resources of Haiti, 1967 - 1973, undated

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents note

1.5 linear feet (3 boxes), arranged chronologically. The papers in this series document the activities and participants associated with the November 1967 conference and have been organized as three subseries. Subseries A includes pre-conference organizing, agendas and programs, administrative details, mailing lists, expense reports and summarized proceedings, which are also included in the CHS Committee Papers (Series IV). Subseries B consists of conference participant files, which have been retained in original alphabetical order by last name. These files include correspondence, curriculum vitae, articles, and manuscripts. Subseries C consists of reel-to-reel audiotapes. Box 4 containing part of Series III Subseries B. Conference Participants was returned to the Reed Foundation in March 2009.

Series VIII. Publication Papers, 1967 - 1976

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents note

.5 linear feet (1 box), arranged chronologically. The papers in this series reflect the efforts of CHS members to publish Research and Resources of Haiti, the November 1967 conference proceedings. A limited run of the book was published by RISM in 1969. As a result of high demand, a second version was published by Teachers College in 1975. The papers include correspondence, price quotations, invoices, dedication page and table of contents, sample cover, application and certification of copyright, mailing lists, requests for publication copies, and book reviews.
