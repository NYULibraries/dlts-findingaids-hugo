Scope and Contents

Series I contains information on the preparation and production of the Asians in America conference from 1994-1998, as well as other conferences of interest to the A/P/A community including Where is Home? Annual Conference for Chinese in the Americas, held in 1997 and Public Displays of Asianness, held in 1998. The records generally pertain to conference logistics, planning, and publicity.

Subseries A: Sheelagh Cabalda, Office for African American, Latino and Asian American Student Services (OASIS),

langmaterial

English

Scope and Contents

Subseries A contains records pertaining to Sheelagh Cabalda's work at the Office for African American, Latino and Asian American Student Services (OASIS) in collaboration with A/P/A. This subseries documents various conferences, workshops and events produced by OASIS, A/P/A and other institutions. These records provide an understanding of student activities and events related to A/P/A Institute.

Conditions Governing Access

Some of the material within this subseries is restricted because it contains personal information. The physical folders within this collection that are restricted and the boxes have been marked.

Scope and Contents

Series III consists of information regarding various events and activities produced and sponsored by the A/P/A Institute. These records are primarily comprised of outreach materials, such as posters, brochures, and flyers. The majority of these events are art-related, such as gallery exhibitions, author reading series, and film screenings. Events also include lectures and discussions regarding various issues relevant to the A/P/A student community. Many of these events were documented either on film or audio. These recordings are included in the collection. This series is divided into three subseries: a series of general materials organized alphabetically, a series of posters, and a series of general materials organized chronologically. The material in Subseries 3 was received at a later date, and its chronological organization has been retained.
