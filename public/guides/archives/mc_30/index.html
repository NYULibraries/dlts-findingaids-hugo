Biography of Cyrus Mason

Born July 19, 1798, near Nassau, New York, Cyrus Mason served as the pastor of the Cedar Street Presbyterian Church in New York City (forerunner of the Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church). Mason was a member of the original Council of New York University (NYU), then called the University of the City of New York. In 1836 Mason became Professor of the Evidences of Revealed Religion at NYU, and in 1843, he took on additional teaching duties in political economy. He also served as rector of the University Grammar School. Mason resigned from the University in January 1850. He died of typhoid fever on May 29, 1865, in New York City.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains correspondence of Cyrus Mason, dating from 1843 and mainly concerning finances and personal matters. Topics include religious life, the birth of a daughter to Susan Hilleron, a subscription fund for Union College, and the debts of Mason's brother, James.
