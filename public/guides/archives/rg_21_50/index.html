Abstract

The NYU Department of Comparative Literature's curriculum is aimed at students with broad interdisciplinary interests, a desire to explore new fields and forms of experimental thinking, and diverse language backgrounds. This collection contains issues of the Department's undergraduate literary journal, Brio and graduate literary journal, Barricade, dating from 2010 to 2019.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Department of Comparative Literature transferred material in July 2019; the accession number associated with this transfer is 2019.083.

Historical Note

The NYU Department of Comparative Literature engages students with broad interdisciplinary interests and a desire to explore new fields and forms of experimental thinking. Seminar-format courses are offered with intensive faculty interaction and discussion. Faculty specializations include European, Anglo-American, African, Slavic, Latin American, Caribbean, Chinese, South Asian, and Middle Eastern literatures. Departmental courses immerse students in ancient, early modern, and modern global literatures, often with an emphasis on Continental philosophy and literary and critical theory more generally.
