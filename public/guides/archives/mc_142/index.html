Abstract

Michael Martin Fennel, New York University (NYU) alumnus, bequeathed $4.6 million in circa 1995 to establish the Martin F. Reddington Scholarship at NYU. The scholarship benefits students with financial needs who want to participate in NYU's undergraduate programs abroad. Materials in this collection include a photograph of Martin F. Reddington, a graduation photograph of Michael Martin Fennel, and two drafts of Fennel's article, "Our Lady of the Trenches."

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Transferred from Alan Shapiro, Director of Gift Planning, Office for University Development, in 1995. The accession number associated with this collection is 95.032.

Biographical Note

Michael Martin Fennel graduated from Washington Square College in 1965. He remained as a student in the English department and received his Master's Degree in 1967. Fennel wrote and published essays and short stories. His grandfather, Martin F. Reddington, one of the pioneers of modern advertising in the 1920s, believed in the importance of higher education. Reddington's passion for higher education inspired Fennel to establish a scholarship fund at New York University to help students in need study abroad.
