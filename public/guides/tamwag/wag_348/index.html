Abstract

Julius "Julie" Wiesenfeld (1913-1979) was a professor of mathematics and union and civil rights advocate. While in Cedar Falls, Wiesenfeld served as the President of the Black Hawk County Union Council, and was active in union, civil rights, and anti-war organizing, helping to organize the UNI chapter of the American Federation of Teachers, and adovcating for amnesty of Vietnam draft resistance while he was a delegate to the State Democratic Convention in 1968 and 1972. The Julius Wiesenfeld Papers (dated approximately 1941-1977) consist of correspondence, writings, and newspaper clippings on a variety of topics related to the Old Left, including labor strikes, union organizing, conflict within the Socialist Labor Party of America, voting rights and voting registration efforts in the 1960s, opposition to American involvement in Vietnam, Marxist theory and history, and international Marxism during the mid-20th century, including the student-led Red Guards in China and de-Stalinisation in the Soviet Union.

Biographical Note

Julius "Julie" Wiesenfeld (1913-1979) was a professor of mathematics and union and civil rights advocate. Wiesenfeld was born in New York City. Herved in the United States Army during World War II, worked for the Republic Steel Corporation from 1946-1951, taught at the Harvard School for Boys of the University of Chicago from 1957-1963. Wiesenfeld earned his B.S. from Roosevelt University in Chicago (1953) and his M.S. from the University of Chicago (1957). In 1963 he joined the mathematics faculty of the University of Northern Iowa (then known as the State College of Iowa). While in Cedar Falls, Wiesenfeld served as the President of the Black Hawk County Union Council, and was active in union, civil rights, and anti-war organizing, helping to organize the UNI chapter of the American Federation of Teachers, and adovcating for amnesty of Vietnam draft resistance while he was a delegate to the State Democratic Convention in 1968 and 1972.

Scope and Contents

The Julius Wiesenfeld Papers (dated approximately 1941-1977) consist of correspondence, writings, and newspaper clippings on a variety of topics related to the Old Left, including labor strikes, union organizing, conflict within the Socialist Labor Party of America, voting rights and voting registration efforts in the 1960s, the American Veterans Committee and opposition to American involvement in Vietnam, Marxist theory and history, and international Marxism during the mid-20th century, including the student-led Red Guards in China and de-Stalinisation in the Soviet Union. The bulk of the correspondence in this collection is between Wiesenfeld and Abe Levine, the first United Teachers Federation Vice President for elementary schools.
