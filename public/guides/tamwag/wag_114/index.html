Historical/Biographical Note

The Civil Service Merit Council was founded in 1971 by Alfred Engel (d. 2007), an engineer who had worked in many New York City agencies and was active as a chapter chairman in the Civil Service Technical Guild, Local 375 (AFSCME, DC 37), and attorney Julian Jackson. Engel became the council's first president. The purpose of the Merit Council was to advocate for strict adherence to civil service examination results, and against patronage, favoritism, outsourcing and other abuses in civil service employment. It is a non-partisan coalition of individuals and organizations.

Scope and Contents

The records consist of organizational reports and correspondence, notices and flyers, publicity materials and background files on such topics as legislation, the merit system, payroll issues, school construction, job titles and whistle-blowers.
