Scope and Content Note

This series consists of administrative materials such as by-laws, executives' reports, minutes, funding circulars, policy statements, publicity brochures, membership and mailing lists, legal correspondence, government forms, conference materials, drafts and manuscripts, logs of cases, bills, and other office materials.

Series II: Correspondence Files

Scope and Content Note

This series consists of correspondence with individuals and organizations. Along with individual correspondents' files, there are files of Miscellaneous writers after each alphabetical category. A substantial number of the letters are carbons of Miss Witherspoon's fund raising messages. Her individual appeals to friends of the bureau and assiduous hunt for donations and pledges led her to correspond with such noteworthy supporters as H. L. Mencken, Sinclair Lewis, Louis Untermeyer, Thorsten Veblen, and Upton Sinclair. The correspondence files also show that a strong network of progressive organizations bolstered the NYBLA.

Series III: Case Files: Aliens, Deportations, Individuals

Scope and Content Note

This series contains correspondence, affidavits, legal notices, clippings and legal transcripts relating to deportations and other actions threatened by the government against immigrants and opponents of the war effort. Notable among these papers are documents of the Chinese United Association, a Chinatown labor organization, and case files on Gertrude Pignol, a New York City high school teacher dismissed from her position because of her opposition to the war, and on the Italian labor activist Arturo Giovanetti.

Series IV: Case Files: Conscientious Objector Files

Scope and Content Note

The Conscientious Objectors files include correspondence with the CO's and with their families as well as affidavits, court martial records, and correspondence with military officials. Subjects of correspondence include lobbying campaigns, prison brutality, strikes and hunger strikes by prisoners, and the progress of cases. There are also files on the military camps where the COs were incarcerated and photographs of CO's at internment camps at Camp Dix and Fort Riley, Kansas.

Scope and Content Note

Most of the individual cases in these files concern the working class clients of the NYBLA. Correspondence with clients and families, affidavits, and correspondence with military officials are included in this series. Their requests for excuse from military service usually concerned their immigrant status or their family responsibilities.
