Abstract

Joan Roelofs was Professor of Political Science at Keene State College from 1979 until 2012 specializing in green public adminsitration, the political power of foundations and the ideas of pre-Marxist socialist Charles Fourier. She is the author of Greening Cities: Building Just, Sustainable Communities, and Foundations and Public Policy. She is also a practitioner of book arts. This collection consists of various papers, articles, notes and fliers regarding the environment, labor issues and the socio-political situation in America during the late-20th century; as well as issues of numerous publications examining contemporary issues in climate change, environmental politics, and energy policy and development.
