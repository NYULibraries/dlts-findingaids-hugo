Abstract

Labor History is an academic journal publishing articles on the history of the labor movement in the United States and other countries. Between 1960 and 2003 the journal was published by the Tamiment Institute, later the Tamiment Library at New York University. The Labor History Records document the publication of the journal between 1960 and 2010 and consist of correspondence between editors and authors, and minutes of board meetings.

Historical Note

Labor History is an academic journal publishing articles on the history of the labor movement in the United States and other countries. Between 1960 and 2003 the journal was published by the Tamiment Institute, later the Tamiment Library at New York University. Daniel J. Leab was editor from 1974 into the late 1990s.

Scope and Contents

The Labor History Records document the publication of the journal between 1960 and 2010 and consist of correspondence between editors and authors, and minutes of board meetings. The bulk of the correspondence is that of Daniel J. Leab as editor and includes correspondence with authors and other members of the editorial board. Also included is the correspondence of Sidney Fine who served as a member of the editorial board in the 1980s.
