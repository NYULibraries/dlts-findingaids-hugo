Abstract

Jane LaTour is a journalist, labor activist, and educator living in New York City. She has written for various union publications, managed the Women's Project of the Association for Union Democracy, and wrote the book Sisters in the Brotherhoods: Working Women Organizing for Equality in New York City. She is a two-time winner of the Mary Heaton Vorse Award, the top labor journalism award in New York City. This collection consists of materials created or collected in the course of writing Sisters in the Brotherhoods, and include correspondence with interview subjects, research files, oral history interview transcripts, and manuscripts drafts focused on women in the labor movement, focusing on blue-collar and trade employment. Topics that recur throughout these materials include discimination, welfare, vocational education, and pregnancy as it pertains to working women.

Scope and Contents

The papers of Jane LaTour consist of materials created and collected by LaTour in the course of researching, writing, and discussing her book Sisters in the Brotherhoods. Materials include correspondence with interview subjects, research files, oral history interview transcripts, and manuscripts drafts focused on women in the labor movement, focusing on blue-collar and trade employment. Topics that recur throughout these materials include discimination, welfare, vocational education, and pregnancy as it pertains to working women.

The 2019 accretion includes correspondence correspondence and research files regarding the #MeToo movement, sexual harassment, sex discrimination in the workplace, gender equality, and women in labor unions. There are materials such as correspondence and publicity from after the release of Jane LaTour's book, Sisters in the Brotherhoods: Working Women Organizing for Equality in New York City, as well as the initial proposal for the book.

Processing Information

Photographs were separated from this collection during initial processing and were established as a separate collection, the Jane LaTour Photographs (PHOTOS 147). In 2014, the photograph collection was reincorporated into the Jane LaTour Papers (WAG 317).

Materials found in the repository in 2014 were added to Box 7.

Materials in the 2019 accretion were rehoused in new acid-free folders where necessary, with original titles transcribed.

Arrangement

This collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.
