Abstract

This collection contains three audio cassette tapes of a 1981 interview by Sofia Sequenzia with muralist and illustrator Hugo Gellert. Sequenzia conducted a three-hour interview with Gellert for a freelance article she published in the National Guardian. The audio recordings of the interview appear to be incomplete and are on 3 tapes numbered 1, 4, and 6. The interview is accompanied by Sequenzia's notes and a handwritten transcript. The collection also includes flyers, programs, and articles from events dedicated to Gellert's life and works.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Sofia Sequenzia in 2016. The accession number associated with this gift is 2017.009.

Biographical / Historical

Sophia Sequenzia conducted a three-hour interview with muralist and illustrator Hugo Gellert in 1981, which she used for a freelance article published in the National Guardian.

Hugo Gellert was a communist graphic artist whose work appeared in a number of radical publications including the Masses, the Liberator, and the New Masses. His is most well-known for his depictions of working-class struggle and artwork supporting organized labor.
