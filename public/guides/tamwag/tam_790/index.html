Abstract

C.I. Taylor was an American volunteer who served in the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939). The letter in the collection describes his experience as a machine gunner in the Tom Mooney Company and his time spent at the headquarters in Albacete, Spain.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Biographical Note

C.I. Taylor was an American volunteer who went to fight against fascism in Spain during the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939) as a machine gunner. While the American volunteers are often referred to as the Abraham Lincoln Battalion, the machine gun company named and referred to themselves after the imprisoned labor leader Tom Mooney. Taylor also writes from Albacete, Spain, which served as headquarters of the International Brigades.

Scope and Contents

This collection contains one letter from C.I. Taylor, an American volunteer who served in the Spanish Civil War. The letter describes Taylor's experience of being a machine gunner in the Tom Mooney Battalion, a name the machine gun company used for what is also known as the Lincoln Battalion. Also included are two typed transcripts of the letter and one illustration dated from 2016.
