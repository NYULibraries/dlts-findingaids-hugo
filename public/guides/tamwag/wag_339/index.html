Abstract

The Metro New York Labor Communications Council (Metro) was established in 1974 as a forum for labor communicators to discuss ideas and challenges of organized labor. Metro offers workshops and seminars to build skills in photography, writing, and web design. They hold an annual convention on issues facing the organized labor movement, and a contest for labor communicators. Metro is affiliated with the International Labor Communications Association, the AFL-CIO, and maintains a relationship with the New York City Central Labor Council. These records contain meeting agendas, memorandum, newsletters, administrative records, and reports on the annual Journalism Awards Contest. Also included are information posters on workshops that were held through the organization, as well as copies of labor-based articles. There are also records of the Constitution and By-Laws of the Council, financial reports, and the Code of Ethics.

Scope and Contents

These records contain materials created or collected by the Metro New York Labor Communications Council documenting their organization's activities and to empower the voices in the labor movement. They contain meeting agendas, memorandum, newsletters, administrative records, and reports on the annual Journalism Awards Contest. Also included are information posters on workshops that were held through the organization, as well as copies of labor-based articles. The collection also contains the organization's constitution and by-Laws, as well as financial reports and a code of ethics.
