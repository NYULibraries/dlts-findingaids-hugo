Abstract

This collection contains the editorial and research files of Bernice Diskin who was the editor of the communist bulletin, "Cultural Reporter". Originally titled "Cultural Notes", Diskin created the newsletter in conjunction with the National Cultural Conference of the Communist Party in 1973. Materials in this collection consist primarily of subject files compiled by Diskin for her research. The collection also contains a number of materials from the Cultural Conference including correspondence with Communist Party leaders, transcripts, and audio recordings. It also contains "Cultural Reporter" drafts and original issues.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Gail Kaliss in 2014. The accession number associated with this gift is 2014.132.

Historical/Biographical Note

Bernice Blohm Diskin (1920-2012) was an active member in the Communist Party of the United States (CPUSA) and the editor of the bulletin "Cultural Reporter". Daughter of communists, Bernice Blohm grew up in New Jersey and was drawn to political involvement at a young age, first joining the Young Pioneers, the Communist Party's children's organization, and then the Young Communist League, its organization for young adults. In the late 1930s, she attended a Young Communist League dance in Ridgewoood, Queens where she met Lou Diskin, whom she married in 1941. Throughout her life Bernice Blohm Diskin was involved in a number of Communist Party activities; she served on several regional committees, as a member of the Party's National Council, and chaired a Communist Party national commission on the activities of cultural workers such as artists, writers, and actors. Diskin worked to organize the National Cultural Conference, held by the CPUSA in New York City in 1973, and began editing the bulletin "Cultural Reporter" in conjunction with the event. She edited the newsletter until 1987.

Scope and Content Note

This collection contains Bernice Diskin's research and editorial files for the bulletin "Cultural Reporter", which Diskin created in conjunction with the National Cultural Conference of the Communist Party in 1973. The first two issues of the "Cultural Reporter" (initially called "Cultural Notes") were written in preparation for the conference while later issues cover topics addressed by the conference and related to communism in arts and culture. This collection consists primarily of subject files compiled by Diskin as research for her publication. It also contains a number of materials from the 1973 National Cultural Conference including transcripts, correspondence with conference organizers (most notably Gus Hall), and audio recordings. Additionally, the collection contains severals issues and drafts of "Cultural Notes" and "Cultural Reporter".

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning materials were moved into archival housing, a preliminary file list was created, and a collection-level finding aid was created to describe these materials. No physical processing or arrangement was done at this time.

Arrangement

The order in which these materials were sent to the Tamiment Library has been maintained.

Appraisal

At the time of accessioning, a large number of newspaper clipping, which were deemed of little evidential or informational value, were removed from this collection. The majority of these clippings came from the New York Times from the early 1970s and had been arranged in files based on topic.
