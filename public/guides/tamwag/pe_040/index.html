Abstract

The Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives Printed Ephemera Collection of Oversized Material is an artificial collection, collected and assembled by the Tamiment Library over the course of several decades. It includes material related to other Printed Ephemera Collections at the Tamiment Library. Most of the material corresponds to organizations in PE 036, trade unions in PE 001, or individuals in PE 030.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials in this collection have been compiled by the Tamiment Library.

Some materials were donated by Rachel Bernstein. The accession number associated with this material is 2014.172.

Scope and Content Note

The Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives Printed Ephemera Collection of Oversized Material consists of large-scale items, which relate to other Printed Ephemera Collections at the Tamiment Library. These items were separated from collections and housed in appropriate containers because of their size. There are fliers, leaflets, broadsides, pamphlets and other publications in the collection. Items correspond to organizations such as the American Labor Party, the Black Panther Party, the Labour Party of Great Britain, the Workers Defense Union and the Committee for Maritime Unity. There are also items published by unions, for instance, materials relating to the International Ladies' Garment Workers' Union, the United Federation of Teachers, and the International Brotherhood of Teamsters. The material ranges in date from the late 1880s to 2014.

Conditions Governing Access

Materials are open without restrictions.

Conditions Governing Use

Because of the assembled nature of this collection, copyright status varies across the collection. Copyright is assumed to be held by the original creator of individual items in the collection; these items are expected to pass into the public domain 120 years after their creation. Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Archives is not authorized to grant permission to publish or reproduce materials from this collection.

Arrangement

Items are arranged alphabetically within folders.

Custodial History

The provenance of the materials is varied. Items were obtained through purchases, donations, standing orders with publishers, arrangements with labor unions and other organizations, exchanges with other libraries, and through ongoing collecting by Tamiment staff.
