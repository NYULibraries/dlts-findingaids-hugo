Abstract

William (Bill) W. Koehnlein was a peace and social justice activist based in New York. Koehnlein was an active member of the anarchist community from the 1960s to the 1980s, involved in numerous organizations. He co-founded the Anarchist Switchboard, a collective project based in East 9th Street in Manhattan. Materials related to that project are included in the collection, as well as booklets, leaflets, independent publications, and typewritten drafts on the topic of anarchism.

Scope and Contents

The Bill Koehnlein Papers primarily consists of materials on the topic of anarchism, including booklets, leaflets, independent publications, and typewritten drafts of original writings. The collection also includes bills, organizational documents, and flyers from 1986 related to Anarchist Switchboard/Alchemical Space, an anarchist space in the East Village.
