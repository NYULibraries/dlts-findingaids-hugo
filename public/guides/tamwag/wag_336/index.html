Abstract

This collection contains the papers of Brooklyn College professor Nancy Romer. Romer is a professor of psychology at Brooklyn College of the City University of New York (CUNY), and has served as director of the Brooklyn College Community Partnership for Research and Learning and co-coordinator of the New Caucus of the Professional Staff Congress/CUNY. The collection includes Romer's papers related to a 2001 Professional Staff Congress-CUNY contract campaign and chapter elections. Materials in the collection include correspondence with contract liaisons from other CUNY schools, reports on budget priorities, contact lists, flyers, teacher surveys, meeting programs and reports, and contract demands. There are also several files related to campaign activities of the New Caucus, a left-leaning political causus in the union.

Biographical / Historical

Nancy Romer is a professor of psychology at Brooklyn College of the City University of New York, and director of the Brooklyn College Community Partnership for Research and Learning. She has served as co-coordinator of the New Caucus of the Professional Staff Congress/CUNY, and has been active in campus and community organizing around issues of gender, race, and class. A developmental psychologist, her scholarly work has focused on the socialization of political activists.
