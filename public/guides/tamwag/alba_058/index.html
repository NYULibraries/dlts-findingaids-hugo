Abstract

Sidney Kaufman served in the International Brigades in Spain from July 1937 through February 1939. Kaufman later served in World War II transporting troops in the South Pacific. He was a lifelong member of the Marine Cooks and Stewards Union and the International Longshoremen's and Warehousemen's Union. The collection contains Kaufman's correspondence, mementos, military documents, and newspaper articles, primarily dating from his time in Spain.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Acquired from Brandeis University, January 2001.

Historical/Biographical Note

Sidney Kaufman was born in 1914. He was a merchant seaman working out of New York when he decided to volunteer for the International Brigades in Spain. Arriving in Spain in July 1937, Kaufman served as a runner with the British battalion, spent several months in the 35th artillery battery, and was commissar of an anti-tank battery attached to the Slavic battalion on the Aragon Front. In January 1939, when most International Brigaders were making their way out of the country, Kaufman was trapped in Barcelona as Franco's forces prepared to enter the city. Kaufman spent the following three weeks traveling the countryside, searching for a means of escape. In February, he finally made it over the border into France, only to be held in a French detention camp for several days before being granted passage home. Kaufman later served in World War II transporting troops in the South Pacific. A lifelong member of the Marine Cooks and Stewards Union and the International Longshoremen's and Warehousemen's Union, Kaufman retired in 1979.

Scope and Content Note

This collection consists of letters and postcards from Kaufman to his parents and others, as well as letters to Kaufman in Spain; military orders (the bulk of which are in Spanish); newspaper articles; propaganda of the International Brigades and the Partit Socialista Unificat (P.S.U.), including a pop-up card; a fascist propaganda leaflet; and other items. Of particular note is a journal Kaufman kept during the period when he was searching for a way out of Spain. Kaufman's scrapbook, located at the end of the collection, contains items from the Spanish Civil War, including letters, postcards, newspaper articles, stamps, tickets, and other mementos. Material from World War II includes a V-Mail with a cartoon by Spanish Civil War veteran Vincent Lossowski and clippings about Lossowski and other International Brigades veterans in World War II.

Provenance

The Sidney Kaufman Papers were donated to the Abraham Lincoln Brigade Archives by Sidney Kaufman between 1977 and 1979 in three different accessions. This collection came to New York University in January 2001 as part of the original acquisition of ALBA collections, formerly housed at Brandeis University.

Separated Material

Audiocassettes from the Sidney Kaufman Collection have been transferred to the audio section of the ALBA collection in the Tamiment Library. On the recordings, made in 1977, Kaufman reads portions of and discusses the journal contained in this collection.
