Abstract

The collection contains letters and interview notes from persons involved in lead and zinc mining describing early attempts at unionization; copies of contracts between operators and locals of the International Union of Mine, Mill and Smelter Workers, CIO, mostly dating to the 1940s; clippings, and mining industry and government pamphlets on mining conditions. The material was collected by William J. Cassidy while he was writing his University of Pittsburgh doctoral dissertation completed in 1955.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by William J. Cassidy, 1969. The accession number associated with this gift is 1969.002.

Historical/Biographical Note

William J. Cassidy compiled these materials in the course of researching and writing his doctoral dissertation, "The Tri-State Zinc-Lead Mining Region: Growth, Problems and Prospects" (University of Pittsburgh, 1955).

Scope and Content Note

The collection contains letters and interview notes from persons involved in lead and zinc mining in the Tri-State Mining District (Kansas, Missouri, Oklahoma); describing early attempts at unionization, copies of contracts between operators and locals of International Union of Mine, Mill and Smelter Workers, CIO; printed ephemera from the union and several of its locals, mostly dating to the 1940s; clippings; mining industry and government pamphlets on mining conditions and a manuscript, "History of International Union of Mine, Mill and Smelter Workers Union Local 15," by Harry Blasser.
