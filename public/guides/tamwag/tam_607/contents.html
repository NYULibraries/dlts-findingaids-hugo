Series I: Research Files, 1961-2001

Extent

27.5 Linear Feet in 26 record cartons, 2 flat boxes, and 1 manuscript legal box

Scope and Contents

Series I: Research Files contains Leiner's research materials on education, sexual politics, and health care in Cuba, Nicaragua, and China. A majority of the research files contain research created by Leiner during his trips to Cuba, including his diaries and field notes documenting his visits, photographs of school children and education professionals, and interviews with Cuban educators, psychologists, administrators, and other professionals. The research files also contain newspaper clippings, conference programs and notes, and scholarly publications Leiner gathered covering topics regarding Cuban education, health care, early childhood, gay rights, women's rights, and economic policies. A smaller portion of the research files contain Leiner's field notes and interviews from his trip to Nicaragua, and newspaper clippings and booklets regarding education and health care in Nicaragua and China.

Arrangement

Series I: Research Files has been organized into three subseries: Subseries I.A: Cuba; Subseries I.B: Nicaragua; Subseries I.C: China. The materials within Series I are physically arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Series III: Unprocessed Audiovisual and Born-Digital Media, 1960-1989

Extent

16 Linear Feet in 16 record cartons
216 audiocassettes
208 sound tape reels
2 film reels
47.52 Megabytes in 132 floppy disks

Scope and Content Note

Series III: Unprocessed Audiovisual and Born-Digital Media contains media created by Leiner during his research trips and while writing his manuscripts. Materials consist of audiocassette tapes, open reel audio tapes, open reel film, microfilm, floppy disks, slides, and photographs.

Arrangement

The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Physical Characteristics and Technical Requirements

Audiovisual and born-digital materials have not been preserved and may not be available to researchers. Materials not yet digitized will need to have access copies made before they can be used. To request an access copy, or if you are unsure if an item has been digitized, please contact tamiment.wagner@nyu.edu with the collection name, collection number, and a description of the item(s) requested. A staff member will respond to you with further information.
