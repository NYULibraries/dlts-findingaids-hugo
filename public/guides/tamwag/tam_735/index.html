Abstract

John Hosken attended Harvard University from 1969 to 1973. He was a member of the Students for a Democratic Society and the Progressive Labor Party during his years as an undergraduate. The John Hosken Papers consist of flyers and leaflets distributed by the Students for a Democratic Society and the Progressive Labor Party on Harvard Universities campus from 1969 to 1973. These flyers and leaflets called for radical action against the Vietnam War, the Cambridge Project, and social injustice within the communities of Harvard University and Radcliffe College. Flyers advertising meetings advocating for squatters rights within the community of Boston, living wages for the service employees of the University, and mobilization for the 1969 March on Washington against the Vietnam War form the bulk of the collection. The collection also includes a press release, contact list, newsletter, and notes on committee logistics for the event that occurred in April 1989 to mark the 20th anniversary of the 1969 Harvard Building Occupation and Strike.

Scope and Contents

This collection consists of leaflets and flyers collected by John Hosken between 1969 and 1973 in Boston and Cambridge, Massachusetts. This material focuses on the actions of the Harvard University and Radcliffe College branches of the Students for a Democratic Society and the Progressive Labor Party. The flyers and leaflets call for students of Harvard University, as well as the general public, to protest the Vietnam War, advocate for squatters rights in Boston, create a living wage for service employees of Harvard University and Radcliffe College, as well as mobilize the undergraduate students of Harvard University for the 1969 March on Washington against the Vietnam War. The collection also includes a press release, contact list, newsletter, and notes on committee logistics for the event that occurred in April 1989 to mark the 20th anniversary of the 1969 Harvard Building Occupation and Strike.
