Historical/Biographical Note

Carl Cowl (1900-1997) grew up in Duluth, Minnesota and joined the Industrial Workers of the World as a teenager. In 1919 he became a founding member of the Young Communist League and soon thereafter joined the Communist Party, USA. Cowl was expelled from the Communist Party in 1928 as a member of the Left Opposition. He was active in the 1934 Teamster strike in Minneapolis, and the next year split from the Trotskyists. In the early 1960s Cowl attended Brooklyn College and became a musicologist. Cowl also worked as a literary agent.

Scope and Content Note
