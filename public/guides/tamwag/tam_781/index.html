Abstract

Steven A. Cohen (1953- ) is an American academic focusing on public management and environmental policy. He is a Professor of Practice of Public Affairs at Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, Director of the school's Master of Public Administration in Environmental Science and Policy, and the Director of the Master of Science in Sustainability Management. Cohen graduated from James Madison High School in Brooklyn, New York in 1970. This collection consists of materials collected by Cohen related to the James Madison Coalition to End the War from approximately 1968-1970 documenting the student group's organizing efforts at James Madison High School in Brooklyn, New York in opposition to United States involvement in the Vietnam War. Materials include flyers announcing group meetings and demonstrations and documents with talking points and issue briefs on anti-Vietnam and general anti-war organizing. The collection also includes a letter to parents describing the group's anti-war position signed by multiple parties, including Selma Schumer, then President of the Parents' Association and mother to New York Senator Chuck Schumer.

Biographical Note

Steven A. Cohen (1953- ) is an American academic focusing on public management and environmental policy. He is a Professor of Practice of Public Affairs at Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, Director of the school's Master of Public Administration in Environmental Science and Policy, and the Director of the Master of Science in Sustainability Management. He is the former director of Columbia University's Earth Institute, and had previously served as a policy analyst for the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). Cohen graduated from James Madison High School in Brooklyn, New York in 1970, and was an active in the James Madison Coalition to End of the War.
