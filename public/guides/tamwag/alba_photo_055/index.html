Abstract

Toby Jensky (1911-1995) was a nurse with the American Medical Bureau of the North American Committee to Aid Spanish Democracy in Spain (AMB) during the Spanish Civil War. The images in this collection were collected by Jensky in the course of her work as a nurse in Spain. The collection includes photographs of AMB medical personnel at work and leisure, including Jensky, Sara Goldblatt, Fredericka Martin, and Anne Taft. There are also images of Dr. Odio D. Perez and Jensky's future husband, Jason Patrick (Pat) Gurney, soldiers at the front relaxing and engaged in non-combat activities, Spanish children, campesinos, civilians, medical personnel and women washing clothes.

Historical/Biographical Note

Toby Jensky was a nurse during the Spanish Civil War. She sailed for Spain on April 14, 1937, with Dr. Edward Barsky's Fourth Medical Unit. Jensky and Barsky had been colleagues at Beth Israel Hospital in New York City. She met future husband Pat Gurney while tending his wounds. After serving in Spain, Jensky nursed at Beth Israel in New York City until her retirement. She assisted Fredericka Martin in gathering primary sources documenting the history of the American Medical Bureau of the North American Committee to Aid Spanish Democracy in Spain (AMB). She died on April 25, 1995 in Sandisfield, Massachusetts. For further biographical information on Toby Jensky see the Guide to the Toby Jensky and Philip Schachter Papers, ALBA #55

Scope and Content Note

Series I: Photographs. The Jensky Photo Collection consists predominantly of photographs related to the AMB's work in Spain during the Civil War. The collection contains photographs of AMB nurses including Jensky, Sara Goldblatt, Fredericka Martin, Anne Taft, and images of Dr. Odio D. Perez. One photograph depicts a group of AMB nurses in front of an ambulance donated by the MacKenzie-Papineau Battalion and the Communist Party of Canada. The collection also contains images of soldiers at the front relaxing and engaged in non-combat activities, Spanish children, campesinos, civilians, medical personnel and women washing clothes. All of the photographs except six were taken in Spain during the Civil War. Three photographs of Jensky were taken onboard the S.S. Normandieon its way to Spain. The two photographs of Jason Patrick Gurney were taken outside of Spain. One is a passport photograph and one is an image of Gurney with an unidentified man. There is also one image of AMB volunteers on a ship.

Provenance

The Toby Jensky Photograph Collection was donated to the Abraham Lincoln Brigade Archives by Max Schachter in 1996. This collection came to New York University in January 2001 as part of the original acquisition of Abraham Lincoln Brigade Archives collections, formerly housed at Brandeis University. The images in the Jensky Photograph Collection were separated from the Toby Jensky and Philip Schachter manuscript material at the time of processing.
