Abstract

Murray Bookchin (1921-2006) was an American anarchist and social ecologist. This collection contains the manuscript for his final book, Free Cities: Communalism and the Left, published by Pluto Press in 2008.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Eirik Eiglad, editor of Free Cities: Communalism and the Left, in 2010. The accession number associated with this gift is 2010.041.

Historical/Biographical Note

Murray Bookchin (1921-2006) was an American anarchist and social ecologist. Formerly a Communist and Trotskyist, he later became an anarchist and a founder of the social ecology movement, which seeks to combine insights from anarchism (and from the Marxian Left) with ecological concerns.

Scope and Content Note

Free Cities: Communalism and the Left (80 pages), is a collection of unpublished essays by (the social ecoloogist and anarchist) Murray Bookchin (1921-2006), with an introduction dated November, 2005, that was edited by Eirik Eiglad, who also provides a preface, in 2008.

Table of Contents: Editor's preface: Creating free cities -- Introduction, by Murray Bookchin -- The ecological crisis and the need to remake society -- Nationalism and the "National Question" -- Nationalism and the great revolutions --The historical importance of the city -- Anarchism as individualism -- Anarchism, power, and government -- The revolutionary politics of libertarian municipalism -- The future of the left -- Toward a communalist approach -- Endnotes.
