Abstract

Andree Aelion Brooks (http://www.andreeaelionbrooks.com/) is a journalist, lecturer and author, including the book Russian Dance: A True Story of Intrigue and Passion in Stalinist Moscow (2004), concerning Bluet Rabinoff (d. 1976) an American Jewish woman who lived in the Soviet Union in the late 1920s-early 1930s. The collection consists principally of research materials for this book, including FOIA material, copies of documents from varios repositories, printed material, correspondence from Moscow, an autobiographical typescript by Bluet Rabinoff, and the author's corresponence with Rabinoff.

Historical/Biographical Note

Andree Aelion Brooks is a journalist, lecturer and author, including of Russian Dance: A True Story of Intrigue and Passion in Stalinist Moscow (2004), concerning Bluet Rabinoff (d. 1976) an American Jewish woman and the wife of Max Rabinoff, the foremost ballet and opera impresario of the 1920s, who had fallen in love with a Jewish Bolshevik spy named Marc Cheftel, and with whom she lived in the Soviet Union in the late 1920s-early 1930s. While Rabinoff returned to the U.S., Chaftel perished in one of the periodical Soviet wave of purges.

Scope and Content Note

The collection consists principally of research materials for Russian Dance: A True Story of Intrigue and Passion in Stalinist Moscow (2004), concerning Bluet Rabinoff (d. 1976) an American Jewish woman who lived in the Soviet Union in the late 1920s-early 1930s, and includes FOIA (U.S. Freedom of Information Act) material, copies of documents from various repositories, printed material, correspondence from Moscow, an autobiographical typescript by Bluet Rabinoff, and the author's corresponence with Rabinoff.
