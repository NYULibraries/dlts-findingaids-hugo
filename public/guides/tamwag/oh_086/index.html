Abstract

The Communications Workers of America, Local 1109 represents over 2,000 telecommunications technicians and customer service representatives in Brooklyn, New York. The collection contains the archived websites of the local and oral histories of union members.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Interviews were conducted by students as part of Claudia A. Schachter's labor history course in 2010. The accession number related to this colleciton is 2010.060.

https://www.cwa1109.org/ was initially selected by curators and captured through the use of The California Digital Library's Web Archiving Service in 2009 as part of the Labor Unions and Organizations (U.S.) Web Archive. In 2015, these websites were migrated to Archive-It. Archive-It uses web crawling technology to capture websites at a scheduled time and displays only an archived copy, from the resulting WARC file, of the website. In 2019, https://insurance1109.org/ was added. The accession number associated with this website is 2020.004.

Scope and Contents

This collection is made up of interviews conducted by students in Claudia Schachter's labor history class in 2010. Participants in the interviews include Irene Abraham, Donny Antinelli, Sarah Brandston, Camacho, Nina Coban, Chris Galford, Robert Howell, Mark Imperato, Iorio, Steve Moser, Cheryl Camella Sealey, and James K. Smith. The collection also includes the website for the union, which contains information on benefits, political action campaigns, news, and their newsletter, the Wire. The website has been captured from 2009 onwards.
