Camp Tamiment, which operated near Bushkill, Pennsylvania from 1921-1965, was an educational and recreational resort affiliated with the socialist Rand School of Social Science. Historical Note

The Tamiment Playhouse was a summer theater at Camp Tamiment. The camp, which operated near Bushkill, Pennsylvania from 1921-1965, was an educational and recreational resort affiliated with the socialist Rand School of Social Science. The playhouse was started to provide campers with entertainment, but it soon became a preeminent workshop for theater, dance, film, and television. Many prominent entertainers of the mid-twentieth century counted Camp Tamiment in their training.

Aura Levitas performed at Camp Tamiment and would later work as a dancer on Broadway and television. Her husband Willard Levitas was a set designer.

Scope and Contents

The Aura Levitas Tamiment Playhouse Film Photographs document several different performances at the Camp Tamiment's summer theater in one film reel and 16 black and white photographic prints. Performers included are Aura Vainio, David Hollister, Faith Dane, Jimmy Starbuck, Kirsten Valbor, Mary Ellen Terry, Beryl Towbin, Genie Pryor, and Lila Lee. The sets of Willard Levitas and choreography of Herbert Ross are also pictured, as is the exterior of the Tamiment Playhouse. Four prints are signed by photographer Alexander Cohen and two are attributed to Lewis "Snappy" Goren.

Processing Information

In 2013 items were grouped by size to better store them, and collection level description was written. No further archival processing has occurred. An accretion of one film reel was incorporated into the collection in November 2017, and the collection was renamed the Aura Levitas Tamiment Playhouse Film and Photographs.

Arrangement

Other than separating materials to place them in appropriately-sized folders, this collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order which they were received from the donor.
