Abstract

This collection consists of one audio cassette containing a recording of the "Irving Howe & His World" Conference hosted by the City University of New York (CUNY). The tape was recorded on April 15, 1994, and only features recordings of the morning sessions of the conference. The day-long symposium was organized by the CUNY Center for the Humanities as a tribute to Howe following his death and featured and featured speakers including Mitchell Cohen, Michael Walzer, Marshall Berman, John Hollander, Morris Dickstein, Richard Rorty, Allen Mandelbaum, and Celia Morris. The conference focused on topics that had been of special interest to Howe during his career, including politics, Yiddish and Jewish culture, and the modern literary imagination.

Biographical / Historical

Irving Howe (1920-1993) was a Jewish-American literary, social, and political critic. He was a professor of literature at the City University of New York, a founder of the Democratic Socialists of America, and a founding editor of the magazine Dissent. The "Irving Howe & His World" conference was organized by the City University of New York Center for the Humanities as a tribute to Howe following his death. The day-long symposium was held on April 15, 1995 and featured speakers including Mitchell Cohen, Michael Walzer, Marshall Berman, John Hollander, Morris Dickstein, Richard Rorty, Allen Mandelbaum, and Celia Morris. The conference focused on topics that had been of special interest to Howe during his career, including politics, Yiddish and Jewish culture, and the modern literary imagination.
