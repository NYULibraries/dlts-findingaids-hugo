Abstract

The Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives Printed Ephemera Collection of Government Publications contains pamphlets and publications from federal governments around the world, publications from United States governmental departments, as well as from US state and municipal governments. The collection ranges in date from 1894-1988. Generally, the countries represented were/are related to communism. For instance there are pamphlets from Cuba, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, the German Democratic Republic, and the Soviet Union. The US national, state, and municipal files relate primarily to economics, foreign affairs, labor, social security, and unemployment.

Scope and Content Note

The Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives Printed Ephemera Collection of Government Publications consists of pamphlets and publications published by federal governments from around the world as well as the United States. The Collection also includes materials from US state and municipal governments. The materials range in date from 1894-1988, with the bulk ranging from the 1930s to the 1970s.

Series I: Federal Government Publications, 1894-1977, contains pamphlets, reports, brochures, and other publications put out by national governments and their various departments and agencies. Generally, the content of the material relates to communism and anti-communism, economics, foreign relations, and labor. Within the files from Canada there are several publications relating to the working and living conditions of women. There are numerous colorful and graphically striking publications from Communist countries, including Cuba, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, the German Democratic Republic, and the Soviet Union. Included as well are numerous anti-communist publications put out by the Federal Republic of Germany and the Republic of Vietnam.

Series II: United States Government Publications, 1913-1988, contains files from United States governmental departments and agencies. The bulk of the material in the series comes from the Department of Labor, the Department of Health, Education, and Welfare, and the Works Progress Administration. Of note are several publications on Soviet education and hand-drawn fliers and reports from the Works Progress Administration. There is also material from the Commission on Civil Rights, the National Recovery Administration, the Office of War Information, and the Public Works Administration.

Series III: State Government Publications, 1909-1982, consists of files largely published by state Department's of Labor. These files include reports, pamphlets, and other publications. The bulk of the material in this series comes from New York State. There are numerous reports from the Commission Against Discrimination and the Department of Labor.

Series IV: Municipal Government Publications, 1903-1962, consists of files mainly from New York City, including a list of registered voters from 1912. Departments and agencies represented include: the Department of Labor, the Bureau of Standards, and the Works Progress Administration (WPA). In the WPA files are reports, a supervisor's and foreman's handbook, and reprints of talks given to WPA executives as part of the Executive Training Program. In addition to New York City, this series consists of files from Chicago and Detroit both containing Mayoral reports on race relations.

Conditions Governing Use

Materials created by the federal government (Series I and II) are in the public domain. Users need not secure permission from the Tamiment library to publish or reproduce materials in this collection.

Because of the assembled nature of this collection, copyright status varies across the collection. Copyright is assumed to be held by the original creator of individual items in the collection; these items are expected to pass into the public domain 120 years after their creation. Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Archives is not authorized to grant permission to publish or reproduce materials from this collection.

Preferred Citation

Published citations should take the following form:

Identification of item, date; Collection name; Collection number; box number; folder number; Tamiment Library/Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives, New York University.

Processing Information

This is an assembled collection. The materials came to the Tamiment Library through donations, staff collecting, and from other manuscript collections.

Arrangement

The collection is arranged into four series. Materials are grouped into folders by government entity and department. Folders are arranged alphabetically within series.

Custodial History

The provenance of the materials is varied. Items were obtained through purchases, donations, standing orders with publishers, arrangements with labor unions and other organizations, exchanges with other libraries, and through ongoing collecting by Tamiment staff.
