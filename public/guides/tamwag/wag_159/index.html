Abstract

The Jewish Labor Committee (JLC) was founded in 1934 by Jewish labor union leaders along with Jewish cultural associations to act in response to the rise of the Nazi Party in Europe. Before and during World War II, the JLC raised funds and organized people to fight the spread of the Nazi Party. Since the 1950s the JLC has worked to facilitate cooperation and communication between the Jewish community and labor, focusing on fighting prejudice and discrimination in the workforce. The Jewish Labor Committee Northeast Region Records document the work of the JLC office based in Boston, Massachusetts. The collection consists of subject files compiled by Herman Brown, the regional director of the JLC's Northeast Region, and dates between 1963 and 2002, with the bulk of the material dating between 1986 and 1998. These files contain correspondence, clippings, reports, and publications regarding the work of the JLC in New England.

Scope and Contents

The Jewish Labor Committee Northeast Region Records document the work of the Jewish Labor Committee in New England, and dates between 1963 and 2002, with the bulk of the material dating between 1986 and 1998. The collection consists of subject files compiled by Herman Brown, the regional director of the Jewish Labor Committee's (JLC) Northeast Region. The files document the work of the JLC in New England, mainly in Massachusetts, through correspondence, clippings, reports, and publications. The correspondence documents the JLC's work with labor organizations, religious groups, cultural organizations, and government agencies to provide training, lobby for and against legislation, and organize events amongst these groups. The clippings and publications reflect the interests and work of the JLC and its partners. The reports document JLC meetings and programs, as well as conferences, trainings, and events sponsored by other groups.

Processing Information

The original order of the collection consisted of three alphabetical runs of subject files, separated loosely by date. These runs contained the same folder titles and types of material in each, and were combined into one alphabetical run.

Materials were rehoused in acid-free folders.

Arrangement

The collection is arranged in alphabetical order by folder title. The inventory lists box and folder numbers for an alphabetical range of titles. Folder titles that consist of a large amount of material are listed individually.
