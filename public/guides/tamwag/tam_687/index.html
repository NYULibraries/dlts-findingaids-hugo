Abstract

The Robert Gruenwald Papers (dates 1953-1986) consist of flyers, bulletins, periodicals, and pamphlets related to Gruenwald's involvement in labor organizing and leftist politics. Robert Gruenwald was a labor organizer and activist in leftist student politics in New York City in the 1970s and 1980s. The bulk of the collection relates to his membership in the American Postal Workers Union and the New York Metro Area Postal Workers' Union, and consists of flyers, bulletins, newspaper clippings, and mailings about union elections. The collection also contains copies periodicals inlcuding issues of The American Postal Worker (1974-1986), Union Mail (1978-1985), The Worker for the New York-New Jersey Area (1973-1978), Revolution (Revolutionary Union, 1973), and The Red Papers (1969-1972). Pamphlets relate to a variety of topics on workers and civil rights, student activism, China, the Vietnam War, and organizations on the political left.

Historical/Biographical Note

Robert Gruenwald was a labor organizer and activist in leftist student politics in New York City in the 1970s and 1980s. He was a student at Columbia University in the early 1970s, organizing around anti-war and local campus issues. Later he became active in the labor movement working to promote union democracy and trade unionism. He served as a shop steward in the New York Metro Area Postal Workers Union for 5 years.

Scope and Content Note

The Robert Gruenwald Papers (dates 1953-1986) consist of flyers, bulletins, periodicals, and pamphlets related to Gruenwald's involvement in labor organizing and leftist politics. The bulk of the collection relates to his membership in the American Postal Workers Union and the New York Metro Area Postal Workers' Union. These materials consist of flyers, bulletins, newspaper clippings, and mailings about union elections. Also included among these materials are copies of postal union periodicals American Postal Worker (1974-1986) and Union Mail (1978-1985).

The remainder of the collection consists of periodicals and pamphlets more generally related to leftist politics, including issues of The Worker for the New York-New Jersey Area (1973-1978), Revolution (Revolutionary Union, 1973), and The Red Papers (1969-1972). Pamphlets in this collection relate to a variety of topics on workers and civil rights, student activism, China, the Vietnam War, and organizations on the political left. Several pamphlets and leaflets were produced by the Revolutionary Communist Party.

Also included in the collection is a 1976 vinyl record of labor songs titled, Break the Chains, by Prarie Fire.

Processing Information

At the time of accessioning, materials in this collection were rehoused into archival folders. Original folders and envelopes containing postal worker union materials have been replaced, but the arrangement and original groupings have been retained. Pamphlets and periodicals were loose in the box and have been grouped by title, publisher, or topic. Several periodicals and pamphlets in this collection were treated for mold.

Arrangement

This collection is arranged in rough groupings by topic.
