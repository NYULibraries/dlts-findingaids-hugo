Series XVI: Accretions, 1944-2011

Scope and Content Note

A large portion of this series consist of the papers and lectures of James E. Jackson. These materials include several recordings of James Jackson's lectures, manuscript drafts, and notes on race and politics from the 1970s. They also contain legal briefs, correspondence (both personal and professional), pamphlets, flyers, and photographs of James and Esther during their travels or at special events.

The series also contains materials related to Esther Jackson, including some personal correspondence, photographs, and memorabilia from the 1990s and 2000s. There are also some records related to the cultural quarterly Freedomways, which Esther helped found in 1961 and edited until 1986. These materials include financial reports from the 1960s, oversized accounts ledgers from 1972-1986, photographs, and assorted promotional materials. The collection also contains some materials related to the Jacksons' involvement in the Southern Negro Youth Congress (SNYC). A number of these materials include correspondence and articles about the SNYC from the 2000s, but other materials include correspondence and flyers from the 1940s-1960s. The collection also contains a small number of black and white Daily Worker photographs.
