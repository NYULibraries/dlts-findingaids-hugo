Abstract

In 1979 the staff of the New-York Historical Society began an organizing drive with District 65, United Automobile Workers of America. Victory in the union elections led to contract negotiations, and the staff conducted a six month strike before signing a collective bargaining agreement in 1980. This collection contains newspaper clippings, photographs, minutes from the negotiating committee, copies of contracts, and leaflets.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by John A. Lovari in 2000. The accession number 2000.029 is associated with this gift.

Historical/Biographical Note

In 1979 the staff of the New-York Historical Society began an organizing drive with District 65, United Automobile Workers of America. Victory in the union elections led to contract negotiations later in the same year. The negotiations were unsuccessful, and the staff conducted a six month strike before signing a collective bargaining agreement in 1980.

John Lovari was a reference librarian at the New-York Historical Society, head of the negotiating committee, and after an agreement was reached, a shop steward.
