Scope and Content Note

The Collection of Soviet Union Photograph Albums and Photographs contains two untitled photograph albums and photographs depicting activities in the Soviet Union and led by the Soviet state during the 1930s and 1940s. One photograph album includes photographs of large-scale construction and city planning projects in the Soviet Union, likely during the Joseph Stalin era in the 1930s or 1940s. The other photograph album contains photographs from the U.S.S.R. Conference for Peace held in Moscow in August 1949. All photographs include English-language captions. The inside front cover of the album contains a sticker with the emblem of the Ernest C. Ropes Memorial Library at the American Russian Institute and signature of Harlow Shapley. Additionally, there are photographs from the 1937 Soviet film "Lenin in October". Small photographs are mostly 5" x 7." Large photographs are mostly 9" x 11" and have "MADE IN THE USSR" inscribed in the lower righthand corner. There are also photograph negatives.

Processing Information

In 2013, two boxes of photographs from the collection the National Council of American-Soviet Friendship Photographs (PHOTOS 054) were repatriated to the National Council of American-Soviet Friendship Records (TAM 134). Due to the unknown provenance of the remainder of PHOTOS 054, the Collection of Soviet Union Photograph Albums and Photographs (PHOTOS 286) was established as a discrete collection.
