Abstract

This collection consists of interviews of women who entered the industrial workforce (automobiles, shipbuilding, aircraft manufacturing, electrical equipment manufacturing, and transportation) during the years of World War II (1939-1945). Interviews we were created on digital video for use in the Spargel Productions new media project "The Girl With The Rivet Gun." Topics covered include gender relations and discrimination at work, union experience, industrial work, family roles, transitioning from rural to urban environments, race relations, and post-war employment experiences. Interviews were taken in English and transcripts are available for all interviews.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

The Rosie the Riveter film project was conducted in conjunction with Spargel Productions, LLC and the Tamiment Library and Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives beginning in 2010. The accession number associated with this collection is 2010.014.

Biographical / Historical

These interviews were recorded as a part of a collaborative media project by Spargel Productions and New York University's Tamiment Library. These oral histories were created on digital video and portions of them are used in the Spargel Production new media project "The Girl With The Rivet Gun." They are being preserved at the Tamiment Library.

Scope and Contents

This collection consists of thirty-three interviews with women who entered the industrial workforce (automobiles, shipbuilding, aircraft manufacturing, electrical equipment manufacturing, and transportation) during the years of World War II (1939-1945). Topics covered include gender relations and discrimination at work, union experience, industrial work, family roles, transitioning from rural to urban environments, education, race relations, and post-war employment experiences.

Conditions Governing Access

Materials are open without restrictions.

Copyright is assumed to be held by the original creator of individual items in the collection; these items are expected to pass into the public domain 120 years after their creation. Processing Information

Decisions regarding arrangement, description, and physical interventions for this collection prior to 2019 have not yet been recorded.

In 2019 the DVDs were forensically imaged, analyzed, and arranged using Research Workspace. New York University Libraries follow professional standards and best practices when imaging, ingesting, and processing born-digital material in order to maintain the integrity and authenticity of the content.

Arrangement

This collection has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor.

Audiovisual Access Policies and Procedures

Access to audiovisual materials in this collection is available through digitized access copies. Researchers may view an item's original container, but the media themselves are not available for playback because of preservation concerns. Materials that have already been digitized are noted in the collection's finding aid and can be requested in our reading room.

Born-Digital Access Policies and Procedures

An access terminal for born-digital materials in the collection is available by appointment for reading room viewing and listening only. Researchers may view an item's original container and/or carrier, but the physical carriers themselves are not available for use because of preservation concerns.
