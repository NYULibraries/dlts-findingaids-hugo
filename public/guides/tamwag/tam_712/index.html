Abstract

The Bob Erler Papers (dated 1970-2008) are comprised of correspondence, flyers, pamphlets, and periodicals from anarchist organizations in the New York area. Erler was the program director for the Libertarian Book Club's (LBC) "Anarchist Forum" from the 1990s to 2009. Through is connection with anarchist groups in the North East, he organized and invited speakers from the radical left for monthly talks and discussions at the LBC or the A.J. Muste Center. Correspondence in his collection relates to the Atlantic Anarchist Circle 1999-2000, Anarchist Forums talks between 2000 and 2001, and general anarchist listserv discussions in the 1990s. The collection also contains an assortment of anarchist periodicals, newsletters, and zines from the 1970s to 2000s, including copies of Erler's pamphlet "Anarchist Booklist" and related distribution materials. Other materials in the collection include flyers and ephemera related to assorted anarchist events between 1984 and 2007.

Content Description

The Bob Erler Papers (dated 1970-2008) are comprised of correspondence, flyers, pamphlets, and periodicals from anarchist intellectual forums in the New York area. These materials document a central intellectual and social activity for many New Yorkers on the left during those years. Correspondence in this collection relates to the Atlantic Anarchist Circle 1999-2000, Anarchist Forums talks 2000-2001, and general anarchist listserv discussions in the 1990s. The collection also contains an assortment of anarchist periodicals, newsletters, and zines from the 1970s to 2000s, including copies of Erler's pamphlet "Anarchist Booklist" and related distribution materials. Other materials in the collection include flyers and ephemera related to assorted anarchist events between 1984 and 2007. The collection also includes an audio recording of a play titled A Musical Portrait of Emma Goldman.
