Abstract

Selma Ruth Chadwick (1904-1962) was a Polish American nurse. She completed her nursing training at Bellevue Hospital in 1928, served as a nurse in the Republican Medical Services during the Spanish Civil War, and later served as a nurse in the United States Army during World War II, eventually earning the rank of Captain. The Selma Chadwick Papers (dated 1928-1962) documents her nursing education and service as a military nurse during the Spanish Civil War and World War II. Materials in this collection include photographic prints, letters written by Chadwick to family and friends during her service, and administrative documents regarding her nursing education and service. The collection also includes materials related to her funeral, including condolence mail and the funeral guest book.

Scope and Contents

The Selma Chadwick Papers (dated 1928-1962) consist of materials created and collected by Selma Chadwick documenting her nursing education and service as a military nurse with the Republican Medical Services during the Spanish Civil War and with the United States Army during World War II. Materials in this collection include photographic prints, letters written by Chadwick to family and friends during her service, and administrative documents regarding her nursing education and service. In her letters, Chadwick describes the types of illness and injuries she observes, explains her decision to go to Spain, discusses her professional plans following the conclusion of her military services, and shares her impressions of Spain, France, and Germany. The collection also includes materials related to her funeral, including condolence mail and the funeral guest book.
