Series II, Correspondence

Scope and Content Note

Series II, Correspondence (1954-2006) is organized into three subseries: the alphabetical correspondence includes correspondence with his attorney and writing collaborator Melvin Wulf, various periodicals and publishers, including Lyle Stuart, Maurice Bishop (Prime Minister of Grenada (1979-83), the Social Democratic Party of Germany and its youth organization, Noam Chomsky and various progressive activists and authors around the world; the chronological correspondence subseries contain single or at most a few letters from diverse individuals of lesser reknown, ; and the foreign languages subseries contains correspondence, in German and to a lesser degree, Spanish, and concerns Agee's literary and political activities and connections. Note that Melvin Wulf also sometimes appears as a correspondent or author in series IV, Government Documents, and V, Legal Cases and Documents.

Series IV, Government Documents

Scope and Content Note

Series IV, Government Documents (1950-1989), contains materials obtained under the Freedom of Information Act, the largest number from the CIA, but also including documents from the Department of State (individual files on countries around the world, including several on Great Britain), the Department of Justice, and the FBI, and several small files from other agencies. The CIA files and Department of Justice files include, in addition to files requested by Agee about various activities of these agencies, also include files about Agee, and about the contested nature of the FOIA process. The FBI files are principally lists of deleted materials, i.e., materials not released, but there is also a list of released documents and well as some documents themselves.
