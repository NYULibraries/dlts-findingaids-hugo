Scope and Contents

The collection consists of clippings and photocopies of clippings pertaining to the Spanish Civil War and especially to Jewish participation in the War. Among them are many cartoons, photos, and other graphics clipped from a variety of Yiddish-language newspapers and journals, most of them published in the United States. Much of the material dates from the Spanish Civil War era; other items are commemorative or historical in nature. There is a large folder of clippings of cartoons on the Civil War and the fight against fascism by William Gropper (from the Morgen Freiheit), a folder of cartoons by (from Der Tog), and a number of cartoons from other sources. Many of the clippings are pasted on pages from a notebook or scrapbook, apparently compiled ca. 1936-1938. The bulk of the individual clippings are unidentified as to source, and undated, but the pages could be reassembled in chronological order by comparing the clippings to the original newspapers that are the main sources for them. Also included are several complete issues and articles from Folks-Sztyme (1966-1976), a Yiddish newspaper published in Warsaw.

Conditions Governing Use

Because of the assembled nature of this collection, copyright status varies across the collection. Items published after 1922 and before 1978 without copyright notice or renewal are in the public domain. Items published after 1922 and before 1964 with copyright notice, but not renewed after 28 years are in the public domain; if renewed after 28 years, protection extends 95 years after publication. For more information, please contact the Tamiment Library & Robert F. Wagner Labor Archives, tamiment.wagner@nyu.edu or 212-998-2630.

Custodial History

The material was donated by George Brodsky to the Abraham Lincoln Brigade Archives at Brandeis University in 1979, and transferred to the Tamiment Library with other ALBA collections in 2001.
