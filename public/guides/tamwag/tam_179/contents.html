Series I: Personal Papers, 1913-2003

Scope and Content Note

Directly related to Morris Schappes' personal life, this series includes correspondence (1928-2002); biographical articles and brief sketches; interview transcripts and Schappes' responses to several questionnaires; items associated with the schools he attended and those where he served on the faculty -- Townsend Harris Hall High School, City College of New York (CCNY), Columbia University, Queens College, the Jefferson School and the School for Jewish Studies; documents relating his experiences with the Rapp-Coudert Committee, the Schappes Defense Committee, his time in prison and subsequent parole; materials describing his many personal appearances, lectures and speaking engagements (1943-1994); a selection of his writings -- articles, book reviews and letters to the editor; and ephemera such as identification cards, his wedding ketubah, citizenship papers and copies of personal photographs.

Series III: WBAI Broadcasts, 1966-1994

Scope and Content Note

In September of 1966, Morris Schappes began hosting a weekly "Commentary on Jewish Affairs" at FM radio station WBAI, New York. The station advertised the program as "a secular Jewish point of view on contemporary Jewish development in relation to general American issues and in relation to Jewish life and cultural expression in literature and the arts." This series contains broadcast materials from September 6, 1966 through May 30, 1967. Topics vary and include such titles as "Johnson, the Jews and Vietnam," "East Side, East Side, A Museum View," "Black Power and the Jews" and "Morton Sobell on our Conscience". There are transcripts of the broadcasts along with newspaper clippings, magazine articles, correspondence and notes associated with Schappes' research and preparation. The series also contains a small amount of material related to other WBAI programs hosted by Jewish Currents in 1981 and 1994.

Series IV: Subject Files, 1911-2004

Scope and Content Note

The largest by far, this series provides an in-depth look at Morris Schappes' wide-ranging interests. It is composed of material he collected either as background for his many books, articles, reviews, editorials, letters to the editor, radio broadcasts and classroom teaching, or simply because the items were of interest to him. His annotations are found throughout the many newspaper clippings, articles, event programs, exhibit catalogs, reviews, obituaries and publications in the series.

Emma Lazarus was of particular interest to Schappes. Lazarus-related material includes manuscript drafts, typescript and galleys for his writings along with related notes, clippings, transcriptions of her letters, reviews, press releases, scripts, sheet music and a good amount of correspondence. Black-Jewish relations is another well-represented topic, as is antisemitism, particularly as depicted in Shakespeare's The Merchant of Venice and Dickens' Oliver Twist. Other topics of interest include academic freedom, immigration, Communism, secularism, the Red Scare, reproductive rights and Israel. Among the many historical, political and literary figures in whom Schappes took an interest are Saul Bellow, Philip Roth, Ilya Ehrenberg, Roy Cohn, Mario Cuomo, Ernestine Louise Rose, the Foner family (Eric, Henry, Jack, Moe and Philip), Sherwin Wine, Thomas Jefferson and Sally Hemmings, Pete Seeger, and Julius and Ethel Rosenberg. Organizations such as the Jewish People's Franternal Order and such topics as Holocaust history, the Bicentennial of the American Revolution and the Bakke Case are also represented.

A significant portion of the series is devoted to Schappes' research, much of it identified with either A Documentary History of the Jews in the United States, 1654-1877 or The Jews in the United States: A Pictorial History, 1954 to the Present. There is also general research material on Jewish history, Jewish identity and culture, and Jewish labor history.

A number of book, film and theater reviews are included, along with pamphlets, newsletters, reports and other publications. Of the newspaper clippings and journal articles scattered throughout the series, many are written in Yiddish and a few are in French or Spanish.

Series V: Audio-Visual Material, 1953-2000

Scope and Content Note

The final series is a group of audio cassettes and reel-to-reel tapes and one VHS cassette. Of particular interest are nine interviews with Morris Schappes. Also included are a number of WBAI broadcasts on various topics including The Merchant of Venice, "Women and Orthodoxy," and Jews and the Black Panthers. Jewish Currents-sponsored events include luncheon speeches, awards presentations and several forums and discussions. On the video cassette, Nancy Savin discusses Emma Lazarus with Schappes.

Arrangement

Materials are arranged chronologically within each subseries.

Physical Characteristics and Technical Requirements Note

Due to the fragile nature of much of the original media, items in this series must have digital surrogates made before they can be used by researchers. Requests should be sent to tamiment.wagner@nyu.edu. (Note that surrogates are available for several of the interviews.)
