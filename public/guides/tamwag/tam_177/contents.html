Scope and Content Note

Series I includes an autobiographical typescript written during the late 1980s or early 1990s. It is in the format of a personal journal and describes Stewart's early years, his political activities, and extensive reflections on current affairs. This series also contains correspondence between Stewart and several friends and political associates, as well as memorabilia.

Scope and Content Note

Series II contains internal documents of the Workers Party and Independent Socialist League, as well as some correspondence. These internal bulletins, minutes, reports, memos, position papers, directives, and proceedings provide good documentation of the activities, internal dynamics, and evolution of the political views of these organizations. This series also contains correspondence to and from foreign comrades which Stewart handled at the request of the national office.

Scope and Content Note

Series IV contains Stewart's diaries from the 1930s through the 1980s, correspondence to his mother and sister, friends' non-fiction writings and plays, poetry, and fiction and non-fiction writings. This series also includes photographs of Stewart's funeral, as well as a photograph album containing 67 black and white photographs with substantive captions depicting town life in North-Central West Virginia in 1947. Also included in this series are materials related to Stewart's memorial service and letters of condolence sent to Betty Elkin Stewart.
