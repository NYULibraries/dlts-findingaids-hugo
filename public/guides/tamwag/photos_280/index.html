Historical/Biographical Note

The Workforce Development Institute (WDI) is a New York State nonprofit that provides programs in workforce, economic, and community development in order to improve the lives of working families around the State. The WDI Art of Labor program provides photography, writing, and art workshops to capture stories of workers across New York. Unseenamerica NYS is a 10-12 week photography and writing workshop class offered to working men and women across New York.

Scope and Content Note

12 boxes of photographs, negatives, and printed material from unseen America: New York State, a project showcasing the lives and experiences of workers. Photographic prints include 10x15 black and white prints and 4x6 color and black and white photos. Box 12 contains a binder with photocopies of proofs from boxes 1-5. Photographs were taken beween 2004 and 2010 in many cities and towns in upstate New York, including Albany, Binghamton, Buffalo, Gloversville, Poughkeepsie, Purchase, and Utica. A project collaboration between the Workforce Development Institute, Bread & Roses, and SEIU/1199.
