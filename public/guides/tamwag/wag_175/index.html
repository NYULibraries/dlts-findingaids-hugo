Historical/Biographical Note

Harold Robins (1908-1987) was the head bodygaurd at the Trotsky household in Mexico City. Robins joined the Young Communist League in the 1920s in New York, and left the party at the end of the decade after the expulsion of James Cannon for Trotskyism. He remained a lifelong Trotskyist, and was active in the Waldorf-Astoria hotel workers strike, the Detroit sit-down strikes, and other Trotskyist-led campaigns. Robins served in the army during WWII and later became a painter in New York City. He died in Woodsville, New Hampshire while writing a memior about his experiences at the Trotsky household.

Scope and Content Note

This collection contains research files and draft manuscripts of Robin's unpublished memoir about the assassination of Leon Trotsky. The research files include handwritten notes, copies of Trotsky's correspondence, newspaper clippings, testimonies and FBI documents obtained through FOIA requests. The collection also contains a small amount of correspondence.
