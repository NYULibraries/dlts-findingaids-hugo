Abstract

The Lee Stookey Papers contain documents recording Stookey's efforts to improve New York City public schools. A member of New York, New York's District 13 Community School Board she wrote a series of reports on public education based on her experiences.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials found in repository; provenance is unknown. The papers are possibly a deaccession from the archives of Teachers College Library, Columbia University. The accession number associated with this collection is 2018.085.

Biographical Note

Lee Stookey, also known as Elizabeth Stookey, was elected to the District 13 community school board in 1975 and represented her district on the Community School Board Council on Collective Negotiations. The Community School Board Council on Collective Negotiations acts as a consultant to the New York Board of Education during contract negotiations with employee unions. She recorded her negotiating experience in a series of reports on public education from 1976 to 1978.

Scope and Contents

The Lee Stookey Papers contain documents recording Stookey's efforts to improve New York City public schools, including published reports on school food, education reform, and collective bargaining, as well as her notebooks of committee and Parent Teachers Association meeting notes from 1969 to 1980.
