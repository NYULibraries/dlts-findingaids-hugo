Historical/Biographical Note

Paul Cronin (1972, London) is co-founder of Sticking Place Films, a production and distribution company in London. His films include Look out Haskell, It's real!, The Making of Medium Cool (2001), Film as a Subversive Art: Amos Vogel and Cinema 16 (2003), and In the Beginning was the Image: Conversations with Peter Whitehead (2006). He is also the editor of several books on the film industry, including Herzog on Herzog and Alexander Mackendrick's On Film-Making: An Introduction to the Craft of the Director. Cronin is currently he working on A Time to Stir, an historical documentary about the student protests at Columbia University in 1968, set to be released in 2018.

Scope and Content Note

This collection contains roughly 400 black and white photogrphs pertaining to student anti-war protests in the 1960s and early 1970s. The majority of these photographs relate to student anti-war protest in the United States, but several also relate to foreign protests and other general anti-war protests in the United States, most notably the Poor People's Campaign of 1968. Other notable topics covered in this collection include protests at San Francisco State University Protests and University of California Berkely, and the "Stop the Draft" demonstration led by pacifics Linda Morse.

At the time of accessioning, materials were moved into archival housing, a preliminary file list was created, and a collection-level finding aid was created to describe these materials. Photographs have been grouped by subject.

Arrangement

These photographs have been grouped thematically and cover either specific events or general anti-war protests. Folders have been arranged chronologically.
