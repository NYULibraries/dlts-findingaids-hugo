Abstract

The Adolf Gundersen Oral History Interview of Murray Bookchin consists of a 10-audio cassette interview with political theorist Murray Bookchin by political scientist Adolf G. Gundersen. The interview was conducted over a period of five days from August 22-26 1995. In the interview Bookchin discusses events of his life, his books and the development of his political theory. Murray Bookchin (1921-2006) was a social theorist and political philosopher who is most well-known for his theories on ecology and his role in founding the social ecology movement.

Biographical Note

Adolf Gundersen (1958- ) is political theorist who taught Political Science at Texas A & M and University of Wisconsin-Madison. Gundersen later went on to work as a research fellow at the Interactivity Foundation. Gundersen interviewed Bookchin in August 1995, and contributed a chapter to the book Social Ecology After Bookchin by Andrew Light, which was published in 1998.

Murray Bookchin (1921-2006) was a social theorist and political philosopher. He was raised in New York City, and was involved in organized labor and leftist activism from an early age. In high school Bookchin studied science and mathematics heavily, but later shifted his intellectual interests to political theory. Although he received some formal education, his education was largely informal and self-directed. He was the author of two dozen books on politics, philosophy, history, and urban affairs as well as ecology. He is most well-known for his theories on ecology and his role in founding the social ecology movement. Bookchin's political ideology shifted many times throughout his life from Marxism, to anarchism, and later to a form of libertarian socialism he called libertarian confederalism.

Scope and Contents

The Adolf Gundersen Oral History Interview of Murray Bookchin consists of a 10-audio cassette interview with political theorist Murray Bookchin by political scientist Adolf G. Gundersen. The interview was conducted over a period of five days from August 22-26 1995. The collection also contains a complete set of duplicate tapes and transcripts of tapes 1-5, and 7. Transcripts are not available for tapes 6, and 8-10.

In the interview Bookchin discusses his early life and the development of his political ideology including description of his work as an electrician assistant, military service, education, and his involvement in both Old Left and New Left activism. He talks at length about, discussing the Enlightenment, Marxism, socialism in Europe, post-moderinist thinkers like Foucault and Derrida, and his theories of social ecology and libertarian confederalism. Bookchin's analysis of political theory is largely tied to conversations about his works, including his books Urbanization Without Cities, The Ecology of Freedom, Crisis in Our Cities, and Post-Scarcity Anarchism.
