Abstract

Rose Cosgrove (1908-2009) was a leading member of the County Longford Association and the County Longford Social Club of New York. This collection consists of materials pertaining to the County Longford Ladies Auxiliary, the County Longford Association, the United Irish Counties Association, and other Irish American organizations based in the greater New York City area. Materials include photographs, artwork, event programs, as well as various artifacts, such as banners, ribbons, buttons, and ceremonial uniforms.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Brigid Connolly Sullivan, Rose Cosgrove's neice, donated the materials in this collection to Glucksman Ireland House in anticipation of an exhibit in 2010. Additional donations were received at an unknown, later date. The accession number associated with this gift is 2011.002.

Historical/Biographical Note

Rose Cosgrove (1908-2009) was a leading member of the County Longford Association and the County Longford Social Club of New York. Born in Dernaferst, Lough Gowna, Co. Cavan, she emigrated to New York as a young woman. Upon her arrival, she worked as a housekeeper and joined numerous Irish American social organizations. She served as the president of the Co. Longford Association from 1982-1984. Up until the last ten years of her life, Cosgrove travelled to Ireland annually.
