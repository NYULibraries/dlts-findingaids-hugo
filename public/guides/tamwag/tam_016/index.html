Abstract

The Co-Operative Educational Instite Association in the Brownsville neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York City, administered a cooperative evening school offering high school equivalency courses. The records consist of reports, minutes, schedules, and correspondence that document the life of the school.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials found in repository; provenance is unknown. The accession number associated with this collection is 1950.149.

Historical/Biographical Note

The Co-Operative Educational Instite Association in the Brownsville neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York City, administered a cooperative evening school offering high school equivalency courses. The students selected their teachers, decided on their curriculum and fees, and elected their administrative officers.

Scope and Contents

The records consist of reports, minutes, schedules, and correspondence that document the life of the school. Many of the letters are to John L. Afras, registrar and English teacher. Also included is correspondence with the Co-operative League of America.
