Series I. Production Materials

Dates:
1988-2010

Extent

9.5 Linear Feet in 14 manuscript boxes, 2 record cartons, 3 oversize flat boxes
98 Gigabytes in 24,739 computer files
25 videocassettes (vhs)
20 videocassettes (digital betacam)
12 sound discs (cd)

Scope and Contents

This series documents 28 seasons of the Irish Repertory Theatre's productions and annual benefits, from 1989 to 2016. Material in this series consists of paper, born-digital, and audiovisual formats and includes promotional material, photographs, artwork, and material pertaining to legal and creative issues from early productions. The promotional material includes programs, postcards, posters, and press releases for individual productions; tickets, invitations, and programs for annual benefits; and issues of Prologue, the IRT's newsletter. The audiovisual material consists of video and digital audio and video recordings of select productions, including George M. Cohan Tonight!, Noctu, The Irish...And How They Got That Way, and annual benefits. Most productions are well documented through digital and print photographs and slides. Artwork includes drawings for set designs and posters.

Series III. Administrative Records

Dates:
1990-2013

Extent

3.75 Linear Feet in 5 manuscript boxes and 1 oversize flat box
203.1 Gigabytes in 57,715 computer files

Scope and Contents

This series documents various administrative offices and activities of the IRT, including the Producing Director, the General Manager, the Membership Manager, and the Marketing Department. Material in this series consists of both paper and born-digital material and includes correspondence, reports, budgets, photographs, and blueprints and floor plans.

Arrangement

This series is arranged in five subseries: Subseries III. A. General Administrative Records, Subseries III. B. Producing Director Records, Subseries III. C. General Manager Records, Subseries III. D. Membership Manager Records, and Subseries III.E. Marketing Department Records.

Subseries III. A. General Administrative Records

Dates:
1990-2013

Extent

1.3 Linear Feet in 3 manuscript boxes

Scope and Contents

This subseries documents the IRT's cooperation with labor unions representing performers in New York City and the work of the IRT's Board of Directors. This subseries includes correspondence and contracts. Labor unions include the Actors' Equity Association, the United Scenic Artists, Local 802 Associated Musicians of Greater New York, and the Stage Directors and Choreographers Society. Material documenting the work of the Board includes correspondence and meeting agenda and minutes.

Arrangement

This subseries is arranged alphabetically by format or topic.

Subseries III. B. Producing Director Records

Dates:
1994-2003

Extent

0.2 Linear Feet in 1 manuscript box
51.32 Megabytes in 1,159 computer files

Scope and Contents

This subseries documents the work of Ciarán O'Reilly, co-founder and Producing Director of the IRT. Materials consists of paper and born-digital material and includes correspondence, budgets, notes, box office reports, and scripts. Correspondence includes negotiations with the Actors' Equity Association, requests for and acknowledgements of financial support for productions and the IRT, and licensing for scripts. This subseries contains material regarding the production of Grandchild of Kings, a production adapted from Sean O'Casey's autobiographies, directed by Harold Prince. This subseries also includes letters from O'Reilly to IRT members and sponsors, labor unions, and directors of productions at the IRT, including Tony Walton and Frank McCourt.

Scope and Contents

This subseries documents the work of two of the General Managers of the IRT, Shelley A. Troupe and Meridith Sommers. Responsibilities of this role documented in this subseries include managing day-to-day activities of the IRT, organizing the IRT's annual benefit, participating in advertising and development campaigns, managing the box office, and tracking compliance with the Actor's Equity Association (AEA) contracts. Material in this subseries consists of paper and born-digital material and includes correspondence, budgets, audits, contracts, photographs, box office reports, meeting agenda and minutes, and promotional material. This subseries includes Board of Directors meeting agenda and minutes and correspondence with the AEA, IRT members and sponsors, vendors, and insurance providers.

Subseries III.E. Marketing Department Records

Dates:
1988-2016

Extent

0.2 Linear Feet in 1 manuscript box
192.6 Gigabytes in 51,099 computer files

Scope and Contents

This subseries documents the work of the Marketing Department, including branding, promotion of the IRT and its productions, development of the capital campaign to renovate the theatre from 2014 to 2016, and the creation of a new website in 2012. The material in this subseries consists of paper and born-digital material and includes correspondence, promotional material, spreadsheets, photographs, blueprints, floor plans, and artwork. This subseries contains the Marketing Department's archive of promotional material for individual productions for seasons 2003-2004 to 2016-2017, material detailing the creation of the IRT's new website in 2012, guidelines for IRT branding standards, and artwork used in the creation of promotional material. Material detailing the development of the capital campaign includes a brochure and statement regarding the campaign, photographs and videos used to promote the campaign, and photographs of the theatre before, during, and after the renovation.

Series IV. Financial and Legal Records

Dates:
1989-2010

Extent

2.5 Linear Feet in 6 manuscript boxes

Scope and Contents

This series documents legal proceedings in which the IRT was involved, financial audits, and renovation of the IRT in 1995. This series consists of paper material and includes correspondence, contracts, court documents, audits, blueprints, and the IRT's by-laws and certificate of incorporation. Legal proceedings documented in this series include suits brought by the IRT against producer Robert Macken regarding his production of video recordings of The Irish...and How They Got That Way and Fathers to Daughters, and a suit brought by Charles Nelson Reilly against producer Michael Alden regarding Save It for the Stage: The Life of Reilly. The financial materials document the production of The Irish...and How They Got That Way and its 1997-1998 tour in New York City and Boston, Massachusetts; production budgets and box office totals for other IRT productions; and annual IRT audits. Material regarding the IRT's renovation include correspondence with the architect and blueprints detailing the renovation.

Arrangement

This series is arranged alphabetically by format or topic.

Conditions Governing Access

Two folders in this series are protected under the attorney work-product doctrine. They was separated from the collection in March 2018 pending a decision regarding its inclusion in the collection.
