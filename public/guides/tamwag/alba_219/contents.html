Series I: General Files, 1937-1999.

Scope and Content Note

The collection includes typed transcripts of 23 letters and postcards written by Klonsky from Spain during the Spanish Civil War; they are addressed to various members of his family, including his older brother, Mac. Also included are World War II letters; clippings; a flyer for a James Connolly Commemoration at Jarama, 1937 (photocopy); a text of a speech by Klonsky in Leningrad, 1970s; an article about Klonsky by Floyce Alexander; documents relating to Klonsky's work in the Communist Party, incoming and copies of outgoing correspondence, including a letter from Ray Bradbury, 1966, one from William O. Douglas, 1963, and a draft of long letters from Klonsky to Henry Winston and Bettina Aptheker; testimony related to HUAC hearings; and Klonsky's speech at his subsequent trial under the Smith Act.
