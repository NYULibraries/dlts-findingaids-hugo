Subseries ID: Irish American National Bicentennial Committee (IANBC),

langmaterial

English

Scope and Content Note

This subseries consists of materials concerning the Irish American National Bicentennial Committee (1973-1978), established to involve Irish Americans in the activities surrounding the 200th anniversary of the founding of the United States. The organization had chapters in several states through which it sponsored events and projects, such as the participation of the Irish tall ship Creidne in Operation Sail. Of note in this subseries is O'Malley's "Planning Guide to the Irish American Bicentennial Celebration in Your Community" (1975) and an index card file of select people in the Irish American community living on the East Coast.

The 2016 accretion includes correspondence between O'Malley and state chapters of the Committee and other Irish and Irish American organizations, research files on topics related to the work of the Committee, and files documenting the work of the Art Committee and the Research Committee.

Subseries IG: American Ireland Fund Records, 1965-2014

Dates:
1965-2014

Scope and Contents

This subseries documents O'Malley's involvement in the American Ireland Fund from its incorporation in 1976 to 2014 and his membership in one of its predecessors, the American Irish Foundation from 1965 to 1977. The bulk of the records document O'Malley's service as secretary from 1976 to 1981 and as a member of the Advisory Committee from 1984 to 1988. Materials include correspondence; board agenda and minutes; the American Ireland Fund's publication, Connections, later titles Connect; bylaws, financial reports, newspaper clippings, and ephemera.

Historical Note

The American Ireland Fund was created in 1987 when The Ireland Fund and the American Irish Foundation merged. It supports projects in Ireland and the United States focused on peace and reconciliation, arts and culture, education, and community development.

Subseries II: O'Malley Clan Association Records, 1959-2011

Dates:
1959-2011

Scope and Contents

This series documents O'Malley's participation in the O'Malley Clan Association between 1959 and 2011, including his tenure as Tanaiste of the Clan in 1993 and as Chieftain in 1994. The records include correspondence, research files, ephemera, and financial documents. The bulk of the records document the planning of the annual O'Malley Clan Rallies in County Mayo, Ireland. Other records include family trees and newspaper clippings on members of the Association in the research files and correspondence with other members of the Association regarding clan history, Rallies, and membership issues.

Historical Note

The Granuaile Trust was established in Ireland in 1986 to create scholarships, research, and grants for members of the O'Malley Clan and others. The Trust closed in 2000.

Series II. American Committee for Ulster Justice (ACUJ), 1968-1981

Dates:
1968-1981

langmaterial

English

Scope and Content Note

This series consists of organizational and research materials generated by O'Malley while he served as national coordinator for the American Committee for Ulster Justice (ACUJ) between 1971-1978. Established by Irish American lawyers and activists in response to civil rights violations in Northern Ireland, the ACUJ focused on educating and lobbying American politicians to intercede with the British government. Materials in this series include correspondence, memoranda, publications, research notes, press releases, newspaper clippings, transcripts of Congressional hearings, and position papers released by the British and Irish governments.

Subseries IIC. ACUJ Research Materials for 1972 Congressional Hearings,

langmaterial

English

Scope and Content Note

This subseries consists of research materials used to inform the ACUJ's testimony in the Congressional hearing before the House of Representatives' Foreign Affairs Subcommittee held in the aftermath of the Bloody Sunday shootings in 1972. Included are extensive newspaper clippings from Irish and American newspapers and magazines, transcripts of the Parliament of Northern Ireland debates (1971-1972), and statements made by individuals to the Association for Legal Justice in Belfast that same winter. Of note are the 1972 platforms of the American Democratic and Republican political parties with respect to the situation in Northern Ireland, position papers released by the British and Irish governments, the Amnesty International Report of an Enquiry into Allegations of Ill-Treatment in Northern Ireland, and press coverage concerning St. Patrick's Day in the wake of the 1972 Congressional hearing.
