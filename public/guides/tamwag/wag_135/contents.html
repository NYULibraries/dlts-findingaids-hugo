Series I: Minutes, 1920-1984

Scope and Contents note

This series contains the minutes of five of the original six unions (Locals 1, 3, 17, 164, 579) that merged to create Local 3 in 1955 as well as a number of other local unions that subsequently merged with Local 3. The locals are arranged in numerically ascending order. Some of the minutes from the earlier years are in English, Yiddish and German (consult Box & Folder List) and reflect the multi-ethnic make-up of the trade. Within this series can also be found the minutes and transcripts related to pre-merger meetings, and included are the minutes of the Baking Industry Labor-Management Apprenticeship Council of Greater New York on which Harry Lorber served. (Note: The 1950-1951 minutes of Local 17 were received in disarray. The precise original order is unclear, but approximate order by month has been restored).

Series II: Officers' Papers, 1946-1987

Scope and Contents note

This series contains the papers of Presidents Frank Dutto and Harry Lorber and includes two slim folders of the papers of Michael (Mac) Sandroff who served as Business Agent and Editor of the "Local 3 News" from 1955 until his retirement. Unfortunately the absence of many of Dutto's papers in the Collection belies his extensive activities. Of particular interest is an election leaflet from 1947 bearing his picture, and a map with the location of his cemetery plot. The bulk of the material in this series belongs to Lorber. In addition to general files and correspondence related to his activities with Locals 3 and 51, Lorber's papers document his presence at a score of hearings as a member of the International General Executive Board, as well as his involvement with the Baking Industry Labor-Management Apprenticeship Council. He was often called upon as a public speaker and a folder of his speeches can be found in this series.

Series IV: Shop Files, 1964-1989

Scope and Contents note

All materials dealing with contract negotiation and disputes pertaining to a particular shop or company can be found in these files. Represented here are Local 3's bargaining sessions and agreements with small family-run bake shops, as well as protracted negotiations with corporate entities such as Drakes, Entenmann's and Ellio's Pizza House. In 1974 fierce negotiations took place between union and management at Ellio's -- a tri-lingual shop. Of particular interest are the leaflets in English, Greek, and Spanish that were generated during the conflict. Matzoh-Noodle Shops, Bagel Shops, and the New Jersey Division held separate collective bargaining sessions and files document these negotiations. In a sub-series called "Shops Closed" are the records that the union kept from 1964 - 1976 of shops that either went out of business or ended their union affiliation.

Series V: Local 3, Subjects, 1954-1989

Scope and Contents note

This series contains election fliers, ballot and campaign materials from the 1950s through the 1980s that offer insight into the evolution of the union. A union label file provides excellent and colorful examples of union labels for bread. Also of interest are the files that document corruption within the baking trade in the early 1960s (See: James Cross; Daniel Motto). Many of these subject files may have been used as research material in the monthly publication of "The Local 3 News." Michael Sandroff's notes for the Local 3's 1967 publication of its history, "Progress Through Unity," can also be found here as can his unpublished memoir "I Paid My Dues."

Series VII: General Files, 1902-1978

Scope and Contents note

Included in this series are the general files and correspondence of the original six unions (Locals 1, 3, 17, 164, 579) that merged to create Local 3 in 1955 as well as a number of other local unions that subsequently merged with Local 3 including Cake Baker's Union Local 51. Insurance claim forms in English and in German from old Local 3, dating from the turn of the century, are located here and represent the oldest documents in the Collection. Also of note are election leaflets from Local 338 which are in Yiddish. Of particular interest are the merger-related documents, including a file on the proposed alliance between the Bakers Union and the International Brotherhood of Teamsters. Included are papers that cover the meetings and negotiations that preceded the 1955 merger, the reunification of the International Union, and the merger between Locals 3 and 51.
