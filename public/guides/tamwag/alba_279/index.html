Abstract

David Mates served as a lieutenant in the Abraham Lincoln Brigade during the Spanish Civil War, and later served as commander of the San Francisco (Bay Area) Post of the Veterans of the Abraham Lincoln Brigade (VALB). He also served on the VALB National Board. This collection consists largely of correspondence received by Mates in his capacity as commander of the Bay Area Post.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials found in collection; provenance is unknown. The accession number 2010.024 is associated with this collection.

Historical/Biographical Note

David Mates served as a lieutenant in the Abraham Lincoln brigade during the Spanish Civil War. Born in New York, he was a labor organizer in Detroit before serving in Spain. After the Civil War he lived in Chicago and Detroit, where he was an organizer for the United Electrical Workers and a Communist Party activist. Later in life he lived in Palo Alto, CA, and served as commander of the San Francisco (Bay Area) Post of the Veterans of the Abraham Lincoln Brigade (VALB). He also served on the VALB national board. He died in 1977.

Scope and Content Note

The collection consists largely of correspondence received by Mates in his capacity as commander of the Bay Area Post of the Veterans of the Abraham Lincoln Brigade (VALB). Along with regular mailings from the VALB national office and notices from the Bay Area Post are newsletters and notices from Spanish Civil War veterans in Canada and in Europe. There is also a small amount of personal correspondence from other U.S. veterans, including Steve Nelson, Robert Reed and Sana Goldblatt. One envelope contains a few original photos and reproductions of photos from 1937-38 in Spain.
