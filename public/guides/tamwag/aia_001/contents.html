Series I. Dorothy Hayden Cudahy Files, 1936-2010

Extent

4.7 Linear Feet in 3 record cartons, 2 manuscript boxes, and 1 flat box

Scope and Contents

Materials in this series consists of newspaper clippings, correspondence, memoranda, material objects, and financial records documenting Dorothy Hayden Cudahy's personal and professional lives, including her career in the New York City Department of Traffic, the Irish Memories radio program, her step dance troupe, and her involvement with various Irish and Irish-American organizations. Materials in this series date from 1936 to 2010. Of note are music playlists for the radio show and materials concerning Dorothy Cudahy's campaign to become the Grand Marshal of the New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade.

Sub-series I.A. Personal Material, 1936-2010

Extent

2.5 Linear Feet in 1 record carton, 2 manuscript boxes, and 1 flat box

Scope and Contents

This sub-series consists of materials documenting Dorothy Hayden Cudahy's personal and family lives, her work in the New York City Department of Traffic, and her campaigns to become the first female Grand Marshal of the New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade. Material in this sub-series dates from 1936 to 2010, and includes correspondence, newspaper clippings, memoranda, and material objects.

Series II. John J. Cudahy Files, 1925-1994

Extent

2.8 Linear Feet in 2 record cartons and 1 manuscript box

Scope and Contents

This series consists of correspondence, financial records, newspaper editorials, and photographs concerning the life and interests of John J. Cudahy. Topics include Gaelic games matches, Cudahy's service in the United States Army during World War II, and his involvement in Irish and Irish American organizations. Material in this series dates from 1925 to 1994.

Biographical Note

John J. Cudahy (1920-1994) was an Irish-American interested in Irish culture and social organizations. He married Dorothy Hayden Cudahy in 1947, and had one son, Sean. John was a president of the Clare Association of New York, member of the Ancient Order of Hibernians and the Kilkenny Association, and was a delegate to the United Irish Counties. He served as a delegate to the St. Patrick's Day Parade Committee from the Clare Association.

Series V. Commercial Recordings, 1957-1989, undated

Extent

28.5 Linear Feet in 9 record cartons, 2 manuscript boxes, and 19 packing boxes

Scope and Contents

This series consists of over a thousand audio recordings collected by Dorothy Hayden Cudahy and John J. Cudahy for the Irish Memories radio program. The recordings date from 1957 to 1989 and were acquired by the Cudahys directly from the artists or purchased during trips to Ireland. A wide variety of Irish music is featured, including both domestic and international artists, and ranging from sean-nós singing, ceili bands, show bands, and popular songs. Musical groups represented in the collection include The McNulty Family, the Gallowglass Ceili Band, the Tulla Ceili Band, the Clancy Brothers, the Boys of the Lough, the Bothy Band, Clannad, the Chieftains, and the Wolfe Tones. Individual artists include John McCormack, Michael Coleman, Willie Brady, Delia Murphy, Anne Greehy, Michael O'Duffy, Birdie Gallagher, Carmel Quinn, Teres Duffy, Ruby Murray, Frank Patterson, Connie Foley, Mary McGonigle, Dermot O'Brien, Jackie Roche, and Paddy Noonan. Of particular interest are a collection of Comhaltas Ceoltoiri Eireann tours of the United States and Canada, circa 1972 to 1989; two tribute albums to President John F. Kennedy and his address to Dail Eireann; and the albums of various New York Emerald Society Pipe bands.

Arrangement

This series has not been arranged by an archivist. The materials are arranged in the order in which they were received from the donor. The recordings are arranged by format, with the bulk being 33 rpm grooved recordings and the remaining items 78 rpm and 45 rpm grooved recordings. Within these boxes there is some arrangement by artist name, but this is not consistent across the boxes. There are inventories for the 33s and 78s, and the 33s inventory contains location information. Both inventories contain title, artist, publisher, and date information, as well as record company catalog numbers.
