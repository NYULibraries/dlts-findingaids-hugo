Historical/Biographical Note

Frieda Schwenkmeyer (1901- ) was a labor organizer from Cincinnati, Ohio. Over the course of her career Schwenkmeyer worked for the YWCA, the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America, the United Electrical Workers and the Henry Street Settlement.

The Henry Street Settlement is a social service agency located in the Lower East Side, which was founded by Lillian Wald in 1893. The Settlement provides social services, arts programming and health care to New Yorkers at 17 difference locations. In 1965 the Henry Street Settlement social workers went on strike for over two months, demanding a single bargaining unit encompassing all workers (both permanent staff and special project staff employeed through foundation or government funding).
