Series I. Publications and Reports, 1965-1991, bulk 1970-1979

Scope and Contents

This series contains publications and reports documenting the Indonesian occupation of East Timor, particularly Indonesian political prisoners and human rights violations committed against the East Timorese by the Indonesian military. These publications and reports were generated by human rights organizations, public interest and advocacy groups, international organizations, political agencies, and independent writers. Some of the organizations represented are: TAPOL (UK, US and Canada), Campaign for an Independent East Timor, East Timor Defense Committee, the East Timor News Agency, Pacific Studies Center, East Timor Research Project, Clergy and Laity Concerned, Amnesty International, and the United Nations. Much of the material produced by the smaller independent advocacy and information centers were produced with donated labor.

Most of the publications specifically relate information and news updates on Indonesia's repressive occupation of East Timor, the state of political prisoners, the Fretilin struggle against the Indonesian military, and U.S., Australia, and other western nations' international policies on Indonesia and East Timor. There is a small number of materials related to Sukarno and Suharto, the first and second presidents of Indonesia respectively. TAPOL publications include newsletters and bulletins of TAPOL's UK, US and Canadian divisions. TAPOL often created background histories and chronologies of the Indonesian occupation, as well as collections of media coverage.

Other publications pertain to anti-colonial and anti-imperial resistance, including activist and advocacy campaigns against Indonesian oppression, protests and global solidarity actions and mobilization efforts, and liberation movements outside of Indonesia. A smaller number of publications pertain generally to Indonesia at large, its political and economic development with a small grouping related to the communist party PKI. There are a small number of publications pertaining to Western Sahara and West Papua. The Western Sahara materials particularly relate to the on-going Moroccan occupation and its resistance. Some of this material pertains specifically to or was created by the Polisario Front (Frente Polisario) a Western Saharan armed resistance movement against Moroccan occupation. Much of the material related to the Western Sahara are French language. The material on West Papua (Western New Guinea) pertain to the Indonesian occupation of that region and repression, particularly the violence against indigenous populations, and its resistance.

These series also contain a substantial collection of reports of hearings to the U.S. House Committee on International Relations and the Committee on Foreign Affairs, circa 1970's. These reports relate to the human rights violations in East Timor and other Asian and Pacific Island nations, and U.S. aid relief to East Timor. There are also reports on U.S. policy in neighboring countries or other nations. This series contains mostly English language material, with some material written in Indonesian, Dutch, French, Portuguese and other languages.

Series II. Subject Files, 1968-1982, undated, bulk 1975-1980

Scope and Contents

This series contains materials related to specific organizations, topics, or writers on East Timor and Indonesia. This series contains a significant number of TAPOL organizational material, correspondence, mailing lists, meeting minutes and notes. It also includes groupings of news clippings of interest to TAPOL and its newsletters from the UK, Australia, Indonesia and other countries. Much of the series consists of press releases, reports, correspondence and other print material from organizations such as the Democratic Republic of East Timor, Fretilin, East Timor Defense Committee, Campaign for an Independent East Timor, the Committee to Protect Journalists, PEN International, and Amnesty International. There are also some files of writings by particular academic scholars related to the occupation of East Timor, Indonesia, and broader regional history, culture and politics. Of note is a file pertaining to Noam Chomsky's activism related to East Timor, including his correspondence with Franke. Box 9 of this series contains a substantial grouping of statements, testimonies and speeches on East Timor - many of which were presented to the UN and the U.S. Congress. Some of the material in the subject files can be found in Series I. and Series III.

Series III. Articles and Clippings, 1961-1993, undated, bulk 1975-1980

Scope and Contents

This series consists of news clippings, popular and academic articles, conference papers, press releases, leaflets and other print material. The majority of the material is related to the Indonesian occupation of East Timor and its aftermath, particularly U.S. military aid to Indonesia, the Suharto regime, Fretilin, the mass killings, famine and deprivation of East Timorese, Indonesian political prisoners and the international response. Some of the materials relate to other political and economic matters of Indonesia, Southeast Asia, and Pacific Island nations and people. A substantial number of the articles and news clippings are photocopies. Many of the clippings are taken from major news outlets from the U.S, the UK, and Australia. Much of the press releases and other print material were created by human rights organizations, including TAPOL USA.

Series IV. Correspondence, 1971-1985, undated

Scope and Contents

This small series contains correspondence between Franke and advocates from human rights organizations, U.S. government, public interest and activist groups, policy organizations, and academics. Some of the correspondence is not directed to Franke, but amongst other parties and organizations. Several of the organizations and people represented are Amnesty International, Ford Foundation, and Committee to Protect Journalists. Much of Franke's correspondence relates to information and updates on the East Timor situation, advocacy campaigns, TAPOL funding, and requests for TAPOL and related publications. This series consists substantially of correspondence pertaining to human rights activist and TAPOL UK cofounder Carmel Budiardjo - her imprisonment, campaigns for her release, and her 1976 speaking tour in the U.S. This series also contains a number of letters addressed to the U.S. Congress, the Department of State and other governmental agencies related to the situation in East Timor, Indonesian political prisoners, and U.S. aid and involvement. Some of the aforementioned letters are form letters from advocacy groups. There are also several letters to the editor of major news media outlets concerned with their coverage of the occupation of East Timor.

Series V. Franke's Writings, 1974-1982, undated

Scope and Contents

This small series contains Richard Franke's writings on East Timor and Indonesia. It includes some of Franke's articles and manuscripts, as well as his reviews of other works on the subject. The manuscripts include a few early drafts of Franke's pamphlet entitled "East Timor: The Hidden War," issued by the East Timor Defense Committee in 1976. It details a brief history of the Indonesian invasion of East Timor, the U.S military involvement in the conflict, Fretilin and the East Timorese' struggle for independence, and calls to action. The early drafts of this work contain inscription, including layout notes, and original photographs. There are also two copies of the final published pamphlet. Some of Franke's writings are accompanied with research material. This series also contains Franke's conference material from the Permanent People's Tribunal Session on East Timor which was held in Lisbon, Portugal on June 19-21, 1981. An oversized poster from this conference material was separated and placed in Series VII.

Series VI. Photographic Materials, 1975-1976, undated

Scope and Contents

This small series consists of a few folders of photographic material, including black and white photographs and some negatives. There are photographs and negatives of a TAPOL protest to be used in their newsletters, a photograph and negative of a Fretilin meeting, and a few photographs of Carmel Budiardjo, TAPOL UK co-founder and former Indonesian political prisoner. Additional photographs can be found in Series V. within Franke's manuscript draft.

Series VII. Graphic Materials and Memorabilia, 1975-1980, undated

Scope and Contents

This series contains several posters created by various human rights and advocacy organizations. The posters pertain to human rights campaigns and calls to action on East Timor and Indonesian political prisoners. Such organizations include PEN International, and the British Campaign for an Independent East Timor. Some of the posters are related to U.S. and international events and conferences on East Timor, including the Permanent Peoples' Tribunal on East Timor in Lisbon. This series also contains a small grouping of Fretilin stickers and TAPOL buttons.

Series VIII. Audiovisual Materials, 1976-1980

Scope and Contents

This series consists of a 16 mm color film entitled "East Timor: Isle of Fear, Isle of Hope." The 20-minute documentary film was produced by Algerian filmmaker Adjili and distributed by the East Timor Defense Committee, circa 1976. The film is about East Timor's independence struggle under both the Portuguese colonial rule and the subsequent Indonesia occupation. This series also contains an audiocassette entitled "Independencia Total: Revolutionary Songs of East Timor and Indonesia," jointly produced by Fretilin and Agin Timur, circa 1980.
