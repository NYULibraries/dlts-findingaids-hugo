Abstract

John T. Gilroy was a member and officer of the County Fermanagh Social and Benevolent Association of New York, and of Ancient Order of Hibernians Division 3 (William B. Burke), New York County. This collection consists of personal material, as well as materials concerning Gilroy's membership in various social and fraternal organizations, including the Ancient Order of Hibernians, New York County, Division 3 and the County Fermanagh Social & Benevolent Association of New York. Materials include artifacts, photographs, correspondence, meeting minutes, event programs, newspaper clippings, and various organizational records.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Collection donated by Joan Richardson, acting as executor for the estate of John T. Gilroy, in 2004. The accession number associated with this collection is 1997.010.

Scope and Contents

This collection consists of materials documenting John Giroy's membership and participation in various Irish American organizations in the greater New York City area, as well as other materials from his personal life. Materials include artifacts, photographs, correspondence, videocassettes, meeting minutes, event programs, newspaper clippings, and various organizational records from the Ancient Order of Hibernians (New York County, Division 3) and the County Fermanagh Social & Benevolent Association of New York. Personal materials document Gilroy's education and his engagement with the Irish American community of New York.

Processing Information

Prior to 2015, a small amount of the collection had been rehoused into acid-free folders and boxes, and arranged into three distinct series. In 2015 the remaining materials, most of which were loose and unfoldered were roughly placed into these three series and rehoused into acid-free folders and boxes. Because of the nature and size of the materials, physical arrangement does not necessarily correspond to intellectual arrangement. Extensive duplicates of material (such as blank stationary, raffle tickets, or dance invitations) were discarded.

Audiovisual Access Policies and Procedures

Access to audiovisual materials in this collection is available through digitized access copies. Researchers may view an item's original container, but the media themselves are not available for playback because of preservation concerns. Materials that have already been digitized are noted in the collection's finding aid and can be requested in our reading room.Materials not yet digitized will need to have access copies made before they can be used. To request an access copy, or if you are unsure if an item has been digitized, please contact tamiment.wagner@nyu.edu with the collection name, collection number, and a description of the item(s) requested. A staff member will respond to you with further information.
