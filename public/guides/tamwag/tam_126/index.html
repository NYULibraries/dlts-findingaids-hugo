Abstract

United Automobile Workers, District 65 and the Office and Professional Employees International Union, Local 153 led an unsuccessful clerical workers organizing campaign at New York University, beginning in the fall of 1969 and continuing through 1971. The collection includes printed ephemera produced by the organizing campaign and student supporters, statements and press releases by the university, picket duty material, and NLRB and SLRB documents.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Materials found in repository. The accession number related to this collection is 1950.024

Historical/Biographical Note

United Automobile Workers, District 65 and the Office and Professional Employees International Union, Local 153 led an unsuccessful clerical workers organizing campaign at New York University, beginning in the fall of 1969 and continuing through the strike of April/May 1970, the election of May 15, 1970, the New York State Labor Relations Board and the National Labor Relations Board decisions, and another election in 1971.
