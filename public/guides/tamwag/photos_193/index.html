Abstract

The Bette Lee Republican National Convention Photographs document protests against the 2004 Republican National Convention in New York City.

Immediate Source of Acquisition

Donated by Bette Lee, 2004. The accession numbers associated with this collection are 1950.183, NPA.2004.085, and NPA.2005.001.

Historical/Biographical Note

Photographer and activist Bette Lee began documenting struggles for social justice in 1980. Since then she has photographed peace work, labor struggles, protests for Lesbian, Gay, Bi-Sexual, and Transexual (LGBT) rights, immigrants rights, and campaigns against racism, among other subjects.

Scope and Content Note

The Bette Lee Republican National Convention Photographs contain 24 photographs shot during the 2004 Republican National Convention in New York City. Captions written by Lee located on the reverse of each photograph identify the various parties depicted.
